Revolution is more than just a "romantic and vital" emotion

Submitted bytortilla onVie, 12/06/2020 - 20:06

Carlos Fonseca, Cuaderno Sandinista, June 12th 2020
https://cuadernosandinista.com/2020/06/12/la-revolucion-es-algo-mas-que…

Recently, Señora Gioconda Belli wrote something that caught my attention, but only because her determination to say things that, when challenged, makes it possible to expose very important issues for Sandinismo and the knowledge of our history by new generations, so even though blasting mosquitos with artillery fire is not a good idea, using this military technique is sometimes necessary, not so much to eradicate the mosquitos, but if they succumb to the fire unleashed, so much the better.

Doña Gioconda begins her text by saying, with unusual frankness: "I was a Sandinista from 1970 to 1993". Congratulations on the confession, not that of having been a Sandinista, but of no longer being one, even though, for her, the offence is to have been one. We already knew that she had ceased to be Sandinista some time ago, but it seems it took her 27 years to realize it. Because during all that time, and as other ex-Sandinistas continue to do, she took advantage of that political identity and the ethical condition that it implies and the prestige it entails, in fact to the detriment of that cause, the Sandinista cause, which she has worked against ever since.

Those others, who agree with her on everything, continue to speak in the name of an unrecognizable Sandinismo, celebrating the interventionism of North American imperialism in Nicaragua and on whose birth certificate as an internal current of the FSLN, when they had not yet left the Party, appeared the proposal to renounce anti-imperialism, socialism, popular struggle and the vanguard character of the Sandinista Front for national Liberation (FSLN); in other words, to renounce being Sandinistas.

Doña Gioconda, who is very disenchanted, continues to say that she knows what it is like "to wait for dreams that never get realized", or to see "the arrogance of the leaders to listen to criticism, the fear, which little by little is watered down, of saying what one feels and thinks; the fear of being accused of being a 'Contra' or of becoming an outcast", all of which, according to her, happened when she lived through "the most romantic and vital period of the revolution" in the 1980s. How "romantic and vital" can a time be for a revolution, when the dreams that have inspired it never happen?

People's access to health and education, the end of illiteracy, the eradication of polio which used to decimate Nicaraguan children, agrarian reform, the nationalization of mines, banking and foreign trade, people's freedom to organize, democracy, the rescue of national dignity, were apparently not dreams that came true for her. How "romantic and vital" can a time be for a revolution, with arrogant leaders, fear of expressing opinions and all those things that Dona Gioconda talks about? But then again, those arrogant leaders she refers to are, for the most part, the self-same ex-Sandinista traitors, like herself, who agree with everything she now says.

Yes, power generates arrogance in the revolutionaries who exercise it, if they allow themselves to be trapped by its oppressive essence, and this has always happened in all revolutions, which are not perfect. But they are the best thing that humanity has carried out to achieve more just, more dignified and fuller forms of existence. The essence of power is reactionary, because it was invented to oppress, not to liberate. But it is necessary for the latter, that is, to make revolution, to end oppression and to achieve freedom and justice, and therefore, for power itself to cease to be necessary.

Revolutions that are capable of surpassing themselves survive, that is to say, revolutions capable of correcting their faults once they have recognized them, as against revolutions that renounce being revolutionary, renouncing the very principles that are their reason for being. The revolutionary dreams that come true, that did happen in the eighties and that are happening now, even though Dona Gioconda cannot see them, just as it seems that she didn't see those of that period she calls the most "romantic and vital of the revolution" (but I don't know why, if she thinks the dreams never came true). But she doesn't say that this was "the era of the revolution," but rather "the most romantic and vital era of the revolution," so at least she recognizes (well, finally) that the revolution is still going on, now in another era, one this lady no longer sees as "romantic and vital". But how would she see it that way, since she is no longer even Sandinista?

Really Doña Gioconda is not just pretending to be blind to the fact that revolutionary dreams did indeed come true in the 1980s and continue to come true now, for the benefit of our people. She is even trying to criminalize those dreams when, referring to the 1980s ("the most romantic and vital era of the revolution"), she speaks of "a dream that cost Nicaragua more than 30,000 dead, dead that were peasants, that were our own Nicaraguan people, that rebelled against the lack of democracy and the authoritarianism that was imposed in the 1980s, but that by putting the label "Contras" on them they taught us to hate."

Romanticism is a characteristic of emotions, which for some people are a passing inspiration and for others a secondary expression of something much more important, which is the passion of revolutionary struggle as a reason for being. What is really vital is even more important, as is our revolution in this era, in which dreams are coming true in some ways even more than they were in that other earlier era. Because now we have peace which we did not have then, and not, as Doña Gioconda says, owing to "the lack of democracy and the authoritarianism that prevailed in the 1980s" (in "the most romantic and vital era of the revolution", according to her own incoherent words), but because the United States government organized and publicly financed a counter-revolutionary army (known as the Contras) whose embryo was the previously defeated Somoza National Guard that had been created decades before by that same country during the intervention of US troops in our national territory, victoriously confronted by Sandino.

Doña Gioconda says in her complaint that in the war of the eighties "we were taught to hate" the Contras, as they were known, for being members of the army organized by the United States from the extinct National Guard to overthrow the Sandinista Revolution. But who was teaching whom to hate? Because it seems, according to Gioconda Belli, that "we were taught" by a grassroots militant and not by the very person, herself, who was head of the FSLN's Propaganda and Political Agitation Department in those years that she contradictorily describes as "the most romantic and vital era of the revolution," the era of "dreams that never came true" and in which "the arrogance of the leaders to listen to criticism, the fear, which is slowly being watered down, of saying what one feels and thinks; the fear of being accused of being 'Contra' or becoming an outcast" were our daily bread, if we stick to her version.

If there was indeed an intention to teach someone to hate, it would have to have been be an intention on the part of cadres like her to teach the Sandinista grass roots and those of us on the frontlines to hate the Contras, although neither my fellow combatants nor I ever perceived that intention or allowed ourselves to be possessed by any hatred, quite the contrary: Comandante Tomás Borge taught us that "my personal revenge will be your children's right to school and to flowers," and the successive decrees of pardons and amnesties for the Contras that were handed down, from the very beginning of the war were for us reliable proof of the generosity of the Revolution, because, in any case, we did not need to be taught to hate the Contras. For that, the terrible experience we had of seeing our comrades fall, or knowing how the Contra murdered comrades they kidnapped or captured, or the ambushes of FSLN militants, or the crimes against defenseless peasants, the burning of cooperatives, schools, health centers, all that would have been enough for us, the murders of the Christian revolutionary activists Felipe and Mary Barreda, raped in front of her captured and tortured husband, the murder of Benjamin Linder, the mining of the port of Corinto, seeing Brenda Rocha with her arm amputated but with her smile intact, the smile of Nicaragua, the smile of the Sandinista Revolution, the smile of the most generous Revolution in history.

But all of this did not prevent us from forgiving and understanding that the immense majority of the Contras were not heartless psychopaths, but rather, as Gioconda Belli calls them, "campesinos... our own Nicaraguan people," in other words (although this is obviously not what she says any more), blind instruments of their own class enemies, as were the Somocista guardsmen, also mostly campesinos, who were part of the Contras. And when it was our turn to talk to the Contras we captured, we realized that they were mostly humble people from the village, manipulated to the detriment of their own interests. Then we knew that, as Che said, our hatred was necessary, not towards them, the Contras, but hatred for imperialism and the national sell-out oligarchy behind all this manipulation and instrumentalization by which the enemies of the people (among them traitors like Doña Gioconda) continue now seeking to spread hatred through lies, blocked however by the advances of our revolutionary process and the combativeness of our party militants in the battle of ideas. If they "taught" us anything (not Doña Gioconda, but our heroes and martyrs, or true leaders like Daniel and Tomás), it was generosity, love, vocation for peace, humility, revolutionary firmness with which we are once again confronting the imperialist enemy and the quislings who call on it for help.

This lady forgets too easily that after the war those Contras, who in fact and as she now says were peasants, became allies of the FSLN, recognizing in our organization the only political force defending their class interests; an alliance that, by the way, was rejected at the time by Doña Gioconda and the "Sandinistas Renewalists", as they have called themselves since the mid-1990s, when (as she now recognizes) she had already stopped being a Sandinista. So the Contras were not for her those peasants she refers to when talking about the 30,000 deaths in the war of the eighties, as if among those deaths there were not also thousands of Sandinista combatants and party militants (many of whom were killed fighting), militiamen, reservists, young soldiers, border guards, regular soldiers and army officers, many of them also peasants. In other words, Doña Gioconda applies to the war of the 1980s the same lie used in the attempted coup d'état of 2018, pretending to ignore that a large part of the dead were Sandinistas. We therefore allied ourselves with the ex-Contras, peasants from the 1990s, opposing the opinion of Dona Gioconda and the "Sandinista Renewalists" who in reality were no longer Sandinistas (as she herself now confesses), precisely because we did not learn to hate.

The ones who learn to hate are those infected by the virus of lies, deliberately introduced for that very purpose by imperialism, through political manipulation by the oligarchy, by traitors like Doña Gioconda  into the souls and minds of some people when their spiritual defenses are down.

And on the subject of the COVID-19 virus, in another part of her article, Doña Gioconda, as a good pandemic fighter, reveals her main objective, namely, trying to distort the handling of the current pandemic by our government, when she says: "Didn't the Comandante and the Compañera say that they were prepared for this pandemic; that, with community health, with health brigades, there was nothing to worry about because they had everything under control? Didn't they say it would be like a flu, like a cold?"

What does Ms. Gioconda think she is referring to by having "everything under control"? To not having any victims in a pandemic? Or to reducing those victims to the minimum possible without, rather, multiplying them by paralysing the country, which is what she and the other "Renewalist" traitors along with their liberal-conservative allies would like to see?

While they were demanding quarantine (that is, to paralyze the country) even before there was a single case of the virus, they were also asking for and celebrating economic attacks by foreign powers against Nicaragua, starting with the United States. To have everything under control is to have at this point a mortality rate of barely 0.87 deaths for every 100,000 inhabitants, and even if the number of deaths from the virus were in fact what our detractors say, we would still be at 11.26, while the country they idolize, the United States, is at 34.05 and there are European countries with a mortlity rate from the virus of around 60.

On the other hand, has anyone ever heard Comandante Daniel or Compañera Rosario say that COVID19 is "like a flu, like a cold"? They have never said that, neither they nor any representative of our government, because despite the fact that the vast majority of those affected are mild or even asymptomatic cases, we will never fall into the irresponsibility of minimizing the mortal danger that this pandemic represents for people at risk, which by the way, in our country are many fewer than in Europe, due to the youth of our population. But we must still protect them, because they are our parents, our grandparents and our historical militants, whom we love and appreciate so much, and whose lessons we need so much. And that is what we are doing, caring for our people, caring for ourselves and caring for others. I have already referred innumerable times to the countless measures that our government has taken and guided in order to face the pandemic with the least possible number of fatalities and without paralyzing the country.

In other words, the very same period in which  Sandinismo has been back in government, but this time now without traitors like Doña Gioconda and company, who for that very reason see nothing "romantic and vital' in this era.

Free health and education, reduction of illiteracy, property titles, "Hambre Cero" agricultural production incentives, "Zero Usury" microcredit for low-income women, accessible housing, a program to repair families' roofs, solidarity packages of food and household items, increases in the minimum wage much higher than in the neoliberal years, collective agreements for workers, gender equality, sports and recreation, being among the first countries in the hemisphere in reducing poverty and social inequality. For Doña Gioconda apparently none of this is "helping the poorest". But in fact, this government is not here merely to "help the poorest," Sra. Gioconda. Rather it is a government of the people serving the most impoverished so they will no longer be poor, as indeed many have ceased to be, and so that there will be fewer and fewer poor people in our country.

Doña Gioconda asks, with infinite cynicism, that if the country was not prepared for the pandemic due to the economic damage caused by the coup leaders themselves, "why weren't more precautions taken?" Another thing Dona Gioconda says is that we are copying the Swedes (her idols, although of course she doesn't say so), and that this is madness because they are rich and we are poor. The Swedes managed the pandemic, using the model called "herd immunity" and largely failed. It consists of allowing the healthiest and youngest population, less exposed to the severe form of COVID19, to create immunity and then protect the population most exposed because of their age and illnesses. The model did not work well for them because before creating immunity, the mild and asymptomatic patients infected the population at risk, which in that country is a big part of the population because the average age there is high. Sweden is a typical example of the application of the wrong model, since precisely because of its wealth and other characteristics (including the aforementioned high average age of its population), it was actually one of the most suitable countries to apply quarantine. This is precisely not the case with us because, among other things, we are an impoverished country.

Our model, as we have already said, is not the Swedish one, but the one seeking the point of balance, which consists of combining the greatest possible social distance with maintaining the highest possible level of functioning of the country. On that score, by the way, there is something that has not been said, namely that with the economic paralysis resulting from an eventual quarantine, as it drastically affects the income of the great majority of people (even with the aid that is given in some countries, much lower than those available here, even without COVID19), not only would there be an economic situation that for many families would be more tragic than the direct consequences of the pandemic itself, but there would be even more direct victims of the pandemic itself, as a result of reduced access to the inputs people need to be able to take precautionary measures and to get adequate nutrition to resist the onslaught of the virus.

But our model, which is Nicaragua's, not Sweden's, also has other components, based on the principle of not waiting passively for the virus to attack and burn itself out, but rather to anticipate the early detection of cases via preventive and education campaigns, which include ensuring the highest possible immunological level with vaccination campaigns to prevent other conditions from aggravating COVID19 induced conditions. That was done in addition to confining patients with symptoms, providing controlled contact care to prevent the spread of the virus, and health control of entry into the country. And this model, our model, which is appropriate to our national reality, is possible given the level of organization of our people and the characteristics of our health system, thanks to this Revolution which Doña Gioconda no longer sees as "romantic and vital", but which remains revolutionery nonetheless.

In short, don't be so concerned, Sra. Gioconda, because in 2021 all doubt will be dispelled, when the entire Nicaraguan people will unite to defeat the virus of lies causing hatred and death, after having defeated the much less aggressive Coronavirus. But the virus of lies is also a threat to life, the very life we Sandinista revolutionaries continue to fight for, those Sandinistas of all ages, not the Sandinistas of one era but not another, or those of "romantic and vital" emotions, or those of show business, but the Sandinista revolutionaries of the movement's militancy, those with the same revolutionary mystique with which those who preceded us in the sixties and seventies joined the guerrilla war and the insurrection, that same mystique with which others went to the National Literacy Crusade, to the coffee and cotton harvests and to the armed defense of the Revolution in the eighties, the same mystique with which we went to the popular struggle against neoliberalism and with which we blocked the "Renewalist" right wing of the Sandinista Front in the 1990s.

This is the same mystique with which Sandinismo has fulfilled its duty all these years by providing literacy training, health campaigns, delivering benefits to our people by carrying out social programs, conducting censuses of people with disabilities, distributing food packages, mapping people vulnerable to COVID19, identifying from house to house people with symptoms, giving guidance on how to face the pandemic, bringing the light of hope and joy of life, always with young Sandinistas as the main protagonists. This is the same mystique with which Sandinismo as one red and black, blue and white fist, united across all its generations, resisted and defeated the attempted coup d'état in 2018 and the same mystique with which the heroic combatants of the Peace Caravan, historic Sandinistas and young Sandinistas alike, attacked the fetters of death and betrayal. And it is that same mystique with which the entire Sandinista militancy will march combatively, consciously, united and well organized as always, to each new battle for Good, for Life, for Peace, for the Nation and for the Revolution, which is virtue, freedom and justice, which is love and happiness, which is Light and Truth.
