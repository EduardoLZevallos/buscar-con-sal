NSCAG UPDATE FEBRUARY 2022

“Whether Nicaragua under Daniel Ortega, Venezuela under Hugo Chavez, Brazil under Lula, Argentina under Cristina Kirchner, Bolivia under Evo Morales, socialism and socialist ideas continue to hold traction among the masses of Latin America in whom the spirit and memory of Guevara remains alive, no matter the ocean of reactionary propaganda churned out of those laboratories of anti-human ideas otherwise known as the newsrooms of CNN, Fox News, MSNBC, and so on.” – quote from an article by John Wight, Morning Star – ‘After Guevara: the struggle for progress in Latin America’ – to read the full article see here

Dear Friends

For the last sixteen years, Nicaragua has been subjected to intense hostility and aggression by the United States. Starting in 2007 when the Sandinistas returned to the presidency, the unconventional war waged by the US has included the funnelling of millions of dollars to opposition groups in Nicaragua, a failed coup attempt in 2018, illegal unilateral sanctions (the 2018 Nica Act and the recently passed RENACER act), and a pre- and post-election USAID destabilisation plan called RAIN that began in 2020.

Against this background, and in the face of continued ill-informed and biased attacks by corporate media, Nicaragua has achieved a degree of economic and social progress that is remarkable. Poverty and extreme poverty have been more than halved, maternal mortality has been reduced by 68%, electricity coverage is now almost 100%, the country has 90% self-sufficiency in food and economic growth in 2021 was 9%.

With 58% of the national budget devoted to health and education, the FSLN government continues to implement policies which put the needs of the poorest and most vulnerable first and which prioritises health, education, infrastructure, housing, decent work and the right to live in peace. And a survey by M & R Consultants on 26 January revealed that President Daniel Ortega had 70% citizen approval just three months after being re-elected for his fifth term – see link here.
