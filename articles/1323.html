NSCAG - Update February 2023

Enviado por tortilla el Mié, 22/02/2023 - 19:19

Dear Friends

There’s been a lot happening over the first two months of the year and our February update is packed with latest information on Nicaragua, articles, interviews and webinar recordings. We hope you will find it of interest and enjoy reading and listening.

We would like to take this opportunity to thank all of you for your continued support and solidarity – we very much appreciate the important contribution that you are making to our work.

We know that many of you will have been involved in taking action in the UK to secure improved pay and conditions. We would like to take the opportunity to express our solidarity with all of you. As they say in Nicaragua, ‘Hasta la victoria siempre’ (Ever onward to victory).

NSCAG statement on prisoner release

In a positive step aimed at contributing to social peace, stability and national reconciliation, the Nicaraguan government decided on 9 February to release 222 individuals who were guilty of serious crimes. They actively participated in the US-funded 2018 violent coup attempt to oust the democratically elected government.

NSCAG has produced a statement on the release which can be found at the link here

Journey of a Native Son – webinar recording

On 5 February, Camilo Mejia spoke at a webinar organised by Alliance for Global Justice. Camilo is a Nicaraguan citizen based in Miami, Florida. He joined the US military in 1994, and in 2003 he was among the first US soldiers to publicly refuse to return to his unit in Iraq, calling the US occupation of that country oil-driven, illegal and immoral. Since the US-financed attempted coup in 2018, Camilo has spoken out boldly to counter the disinformation campaign against his native Nicaragua. He recently returned to the US after spending 7 weeks in Nicaragua. A recording of the webinar can be found here
