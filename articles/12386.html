even from their open whisky jars

Sandino would rush them, then wait,

Sandino was the night that fell

and the glare off the sea that killed them,

Sandino was a rifle firing hope.

from West Point where the teaching was clean:

they never taught them at school

that the one out there killing could also die:

the Americans did not learn

that we love our beloved, impoverished land

and that we will defend the banners

created with pain and love.

They may not have learned that in Philadelphia

but they learned it in blood in Nicaragua:

there the captain of the people was waiting:

his name was Augusto C. Sandino.

stupendous as a flame

bestowing us the light and the fire

to continue his battles".
