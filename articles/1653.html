Remarks by Rosario, Vice President of Nicaragua,
after the message of President Comandante Daniel to the People of Nicaragua

April 19th 2023

How are you? How are you, Juan? Good evening, and a warm embrace to all Nicaragua's families, fraternally we give each other Peace. Today, after that intense historical journey that our Comandante Daniel, our President, has made, what more should I say? One can always add something more, first of all, to remember how all Nicaraguans suffered in the face of this criminal assault on Peace and the Will of a People that has always fought for Peace, because we have wanted Peace.

To remember the families, the mothers who were truly terrorized by that evil and diabolical intention to mount a coup against us as a State, a State that belongs to the People, that serves the People, to strike us as a People. To kill, to destroy, to stop development, to stop well-being. Because, of course, it is selfishness that prevails among the servants of the Empire... Sheer selfishness! They have never cared how the poor or impoverished families of this Nicaragua live, they have never cared, they have always thought only about themselves and how to better serve the imperialists of the Earth, believing that they will be paid more.

These are the hours we are re-living, with painful memories, the nights when we received so many calls from so many grieving mothers who asked us to do something to stop the bloodbath. It was a massacre, one also filmed and reproduced with pleasure. How they enjoyed the images of the horror they created themselves. This is a disease of the soul. Hatred is sick. Really, a pathology of the heart.

It is difficult to understand Human Beings who torture, who burn, who threaten, who destroy, who terrorize and film everything to enjoy their reproduction, seeking to terrorize more and more. Sick! Poor creatures! And how after that diabolical interval, how they have had to flee their country, how horrible, because no one here wants violence and no one here wants the promoters of violence.

Comandante Daniel says: I don't carry hatred, I forgive... I think we all forgive, because we are not hateful beings, the hard thing is to forget, and I'm frank, I'm sincere: One can't forget! What happened in Nicaragua hurts, and especially the behavior of those who proclaimed themselves as emissaries or intermediaries of Christ hurts. It hurts because one was brought up with Christian, Catholic Values, the majority in Nicaragua was Catholic, but now there is another reality.

How it hurts that adulteration, that manipulation, that desecration of symbols that are and should be symbols of Love, of Redemption, of Salvation, of Peace, of Justice. Of course it hurt us and it's hard to forget that pain. One is not filled with hatred, but one does not forget and that is precisely the strength we have: Not-forgetting means No-Repetition, and that is why each of us is a fervent Defender of Peace.

And when one sees the misfortune in which the same traitorous sell-out plunged themselves , because everything is the result of their actions; every action has a reaction, and truly, Love is paid with Love, but Hatred is unrecognized and so it is a matter of distancing it, of dispersing it, so that it never fills the heart of anyone in this blessed nation again.

They must suffer, and no one is glad, of course, no one can be glad about anyone's suffering, but well, enough of that for now, enough of living thinking that hatred may bathe this blessed nation in blood again... Stop it at once! I say to all the people who made that mistake with Nicaragua: Enough is enough!

We are a People of values, of a culture of Peace, Brave, Courageous... Defenders of our own, it's true. Of course, defenders of our lands that are sacred to us. Of course, and above all we are able to expel, as we have been able to, those who seek to dominate us, subjugate us and plunder us. That's true!

But that does not mean that we live with bitterness, with an sour or super-acidic Heart... No! There is Joy here. It is amazing to see how every weekend, in the moments that we have to rest or enjoy recreation, everywhere is filled, and with Joy is filled, and how we celebrate all those traditional holidays that are originally religious holidays and that are celebrated with Joy, in different ways throughout the Country. And even so they say that we are atheists.

And always in our homes we are full of those symbols of the faith in which we grew up, our sacred images, our candles, our novenas, our prayers, our music, our songs always. All that, the sum of all that, fills this country with blessed strength, with bliss.

I say again that it hurt us and it still hurts us when we see People who claim to represent Christ, but who profane Christian values, who are really moved by selfishness, by greed, by the desire for prominence, and it's not even a religious prominence, it's a political prominence.

What we experienced that day at the Casa de los Pueblos was truly incredible. To see those faces and to see that voice, a voice that came out of mental and spiritual inadequates... Complete inadequacy! A voice and a person who wanted to take ownership of this country, like that song: surely a gringo song, about an owner in this country. That song we used to sing in the 80s, or even before. A person who believed he owned this country and believed he had the right to insult the legitimately elected authorities, who had worked to improve the living conditions of Nicaraguan families.

And the truth is that, at that moment, characters like that set themselves up as the leaders or chief promoters of the criminal coup attempt, and that is what is incredible, inconceivable and painful, I will not say unforgivable, because we have the obligation, as Christians, to forgive, but we also have the duty not to forget, and I will never forget the pain of so many families who wrote to us, who called us by phone, and who told us: We feel helpless because the Police are in their stations.

What terrible things the same people did who thought they owned this country, asking for the police to stay in their stations, so as to make it easier for criminals to go about, burning, killing, torturing and reproducin their crimes and misdeeds on the so-called social networks, those networks of mass destruction.

How are we going to forget that image of the heroine Amada Pineda's son, burning in the street? How are we going to forget? How are we going to forget the compañero Gabriel de Jesús Vado burning in the street? And how are we going to forget the voice of that infamous priest, saying: This is Padre So-and-So, go throw that man's body in a latrine? What a brute! In other words, trying to hide the crime, the crime that they incited, the crimes which they promoted.

How are we going to forget the images of priests blessing the barricades of Death, the places of torture, the places where the People's right to move about, to work, to live, the very Right to Life was blocked? And they carried the sacrosanct images of Christ, of Our Mother Mary, and placed them at the roadblocks... What a sacrilege! That is sacrilege, that is desecration, and that is a curse, because that curse falls on the authors, the promoters, the instigators and the leaders of so much crime.

That's why the pain of those days, not just the pain of one person, of oneself as a sensitive being, it was the pain of so many brothers and sisters who called us, the mothers, and I'm not going to say names, but even mothers of priests, who called us terrified by the noise of the mortars and gunfire and weapons with which they intended to destroy the Nicaraguan people. Those who have always wanted to take over Nicaragua, the same Empires.
