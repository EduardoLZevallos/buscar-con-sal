NSCAG DECEMBER UPDATE

Dear Friends

This is our last update for the year. We wish you, your colleagues and families all the very best for the festive season and a happy and peaceful New Year. And thanks again for your continued support and solidarity.

We’ll be back in 2023 to provide you with more updates and news about developments in Nicaragua. We are always happy to speak to regional and branch meetings, so do please get in touch if you would like to invite us next year. And please spread the word to other branches who may not be affiliated to us already – we are funded almost entirely from trade union contributions, so branch affiliations and trade union support are really important to us. Nicaragua’s National Budget 2023 The Nicaraguan budget for 2023, approved by the National Assembly, maintains social investment to eradicate poverty and benefit the country’s poorest sectors. The National Plan to Fight Poverty remains unchanged and 56.4% of the budget will go towards social expenditure, with significant resources allocated to social infrastructure investment in health, education, water and sanitation, transportation and the promotion of sport. Nine per cent of the 2023 budget (50% more than in the previous year) will go to the implementation of an inclusive housing policy which combines subsidy and long-term financing and has proved effective and efficient in reducing the country’s housing deficit. The budget also reaffirms the political will of the government to continue to defend women’s rights and to promote the development and socio-economic wellbeing of the Caribbean coast’s population.

Minimum wage agreement signed for Nicaragua’s free trade zone Employers, workers and representatives of the Nicaraguan Ministry of Labour (MITRAB) have signed a Tripartite Agreement which guarantees that for the next five years people working in the companies of the free trade zone sector in Nicaragua will enjoy a percentak ge salary increase of 8% for the years 2023 and 2024, 7% in the year 2025 and finally 6.7% in the years 2026 and 2027. The agreed increases will be effective as of 1st January of each respective year and the agreement is expected to benefit more than 142,00 workers.
