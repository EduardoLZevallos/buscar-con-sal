President Comandante Daniel Ortega in the Tribute to Commemorate the Birth and Transit to Immortality of Nicaragua’s National Hero, General Benjamin Zeledon.

October 4th 2019

Beloved Nicaraguan sisters and brothers; beloved Nicaraguan Families... Zeledón, Benjamín Zeledón, today October 4th 2019 is the 107th anniversary of your Transit to Immortality.

Beloved sister; Rosario, Vice President of the Republic; General Julio César Avilés, Commander in Chief of the Army; much loved brother Gustavo Porras, President of the National Assembly; much loved brother Francisco Díaz, First Commissioner of the National Police; much loved brothers Bayardo Ramón Rodríguez Ruiz and Marvin Elías Corrales Rodríguez of the Army of Nicaragua, and brothers Ramón Avellán and Adolfo Marenco, General Commissioners of the National Police.

Youth. Youth, boys, girls, who are the Present and Future of Zeledón.

Beloved brothers and sisters, beloved comrades, Zeledón himself traced the guiding thread of the defeated yankee expansionism led by (William) Walker. He stated it explicitly, mentioning by name Byron Cole, who led the pro-slavery forces that had landed in Nicaragua, when Walker had already named himself President. Zeledón called it : the Defeat of Expansionism. The defeat of U.S. Imperialism fought back then in our homeland, and then later he, Zeledón, confronted the Yankee troops.

And history repeats itself, because who brought Walker here? Who appealed to the Invader? Nicaraguan politicians who had no concept of Homeland, no sense of Dignity, of Dignity for Nicaragua’s citizens. The self-same people, independently of their partisan banners, because Walker and Byron Cole were brought by them and also brought by them were those who invaded Nicaragua after giving Zelaya an ultimatum. Of course they gave the ultimatum to Zelaya with the blessing and the applause and complicity of those very traitors.

Zeledón rose up against what was the Yankee intervention after the U.S. government, through its representative, Secretary of State Philander Knox, sent the famous Knox Note to Zelaya. Zeledón did not accept that. He could not accept it. He said it clearly, expressed it clearly, and he rebelled against this violation of Nicaragua's sovereignty, and he said as much to the traitors, the traitors who had promoted and encouraged the coup against Zelaya. Then when Zeledón rose up in defense of Nicaragua and was defeating the army of traitors and quislings, to avoid defeat, the traitors appeal to the Yankee troops, and the Yankee troops began to disembark in the port of Corinto.

Zeledón knew he was now facing an unequal struggle, because that was when the Yankee troops who were the determining, strategic force confronting the patriots led by Zeledón, that was when they joined forces, with the troops of the quislings.

He knew he had no alternative but to fight on in those totally unequal conditions. He was so convinced of his fate that he wrote as much to his family, his wife, his children, and made it known to Nicaragua’s people. So he, along with other heroic Nicaraguans, because we cannot forget that with Zeledón there fought Nicaraguans  who neither surrendered nor sold out and they too gave up their lives.

And when Zeledón and his men were resisting the traitors’ forces, the gringo troops arrived with all their might, with their cannons, with their machine guns. And then, already wounded, Zeledón was murdered; already wounded at a checkpoint where troops of the traitors communicated with the command of the North American troops, of the Yankee troops, who, just as with Sandino, decided to murder him, with one senior Yankee officer recommending he be hanged.

History repeats itself later with Sandino. Sandino, a boy at the time, remembered Zeledón. And Sandino, still a boy, had his conscience awakened when they dragged Zeledón's corpse through the streets, showing it off as a trophy. Sandino remembered that and expressed his rejection, his condemnation of the traitors and the Yankee troops that had invaded our territory.

History repeats itself. Because Zeledón was murdered by malevolent Nicaraguan traitors in the service of Yankee troops. After the fighting in El Coyotepe,  Zeledón was already wounded and those who murdered him were the misbegotten children of Nicaragua. And with Sandino, after Sandino expelled  the Yankee troops, who executed the order to assassinate Sandino? Also a misbegotten Nicaraguan : Somoza, who ambushed Sandino following a the dinner offered by the then President, with the Peace Agreement already signed.

Peace had been signed! But they don't respect Agreements, although they talk about Dialogue. They have always been like that. They talk about Dialogue, they sign Agreements, and in the end they don't recognize the Agreements. Why? Because the Empire and the traitors are children of the devil, they are children of Cain. They cannot be believed at all!

And History is repeating itself again. We see the parade of traitors, how their journeys to the US increased and their financing, so that they are traveling continuously to ask to for more aggression against the Nicaraguan People. And they do it quietly, and they take photos there with representatives of the U.S. Government, and they insist on showing the photos here, boasting that they are the ones chosen by the Yankee to be the future President of Nicaragua, or to be the future Government of Nicaragua.

And the financing comes from the U.S. people's taxes. I don't know to whom they will render accounts, those people in the United States who organize these campaigns to portray our country as one in which there is neither Peace nor Stability. That is the image they are selling. If a crime is committed here, it is immediately attributed to the government, to the authorities, when Nicaragua is the safest country with the lowest homicide rate here in the region. But the manipulation is crude and full of fury, full of evil.

What does this tell us? That history keeps repeating itself. The difference now, is that after July 19th 1979, passing through the 17 years in which the Sandinistas were out of government, on July 19th 1979, here, in the heart of the majority of Nicaraguans, in the majority of rural families and urban workers, of youth, of professional people, there has taken root a new Consciousness. At long last, Nicaragua after 1979 has a people full of awareness and love for their Homeland.

Of course, the traitors did not disappear, and the Empire is also feeding them, sending them resources through programs they say they are programs to promote Culture, to promote Education ... In fact they are Programs to promote hatred! And, in addition, a good part of those programs resources are channelled through some NGOs... Tremendous business! Why? Because what they receive that's meant to be food for the people, they send to the markets, where they sell it. Non-governmental organizations of every kind, of every political and religious allegiance, send resources to the markets, but they want exemptions for everything, and that has to be reviewed very well, it has to be controlled very well.

If food does arrive here for people who need food, like the food packages that the Sandinista Youth are delivering to people, well, that has to be verified, you have to certify it, because the way they have been working so far is simply to say "the aid is food". Or, for example, vehicles or household appliances and other items, or medicines, or medical equipment that also end up in the markets.

We have to review all that, because, on the one hand they are not paying taxes. And on this day when all our People, when I say all our People, I speak of those who truly feel Nicaraguan and act like Nicaraguans because they love Nicaragua and are against any outrage, like the so-called Sanctions, which they impose on the economies of our Peoples.

In other words, when a People acts with dignity, then the aggression starts, the so-called sanctions or misnamed sanctions, which primarily affect the very poorest and do nothing more than cause unemployment. When the opposition couldn't get what they wanted, nor will they ever, then what did they do? Why did they do such enormous damage to the economy? They committed a crime! A crime causing the death of Nicaraguan brothers and sisters, a crime causing enormous damage to the Nicaraguan economy. If anyone should be taken before an International Criminal Court somewhere, it should be the criminals who promoted those crimes!

And once we had good documentation we went with all that data, all those figures, all that evidence, we went to the International Court of Justice in The Hague. And when we told our friends, friendly Peoples or friendly Governments, that we were going to take this step, there was skepticism, because it was felt the Yankees could not be beaten in the Court.

I remember Father Miguel D´Escoto, with all his optimism, with all his stength, saying: “We’ll win! We will win!” And we went to the Court, and we accused the entity responsible for those crimes, although of course there was co-responsibility involving the Nicaraguans who had collaborated, but the greatest responsibility lay in the terrorist policy of the U.S. government. Then the Court condemned the United States and ordered them to stop all the acts of terrorism they were committing against Nicaragua; and it ordered them to indemnify Nicaragua and that debt is still pending. One day, of course, yes, one day the United States will have to pay the debt it has pending with Nicaragua!

Therefore, we have to strengthen the legal and juridical area in order to fight this battle, such that this will allow the malevolent Nicaraguans who adopt these attitudes of betrayal of their People, of betrayal of their Homeland, to come to their senses. Because always, always, and we know this very well, the biggest problem is in the expansionist and interventionist policy of the United States.

This afternoon I am going to read here some paragraphs of the Letter of the Bishop of León, back in the year 1912, mind you, Bishop of León, in the year 1912, Simeón Pereira and Castellón. He was an extraordinary person, in that time of obscurantism, an extraordinary Bishop who had an immense Love for Nicaragua. He had been born in Estelí, and ended up being Bishop of León.

Under Zelaya's government they clashed, because Zelaya's government was a government that pushed for laicism with great force, and they clashed. But that didn't cause him, it didn't lead the Bishop to appeal for sanctions against Nicaragua. He did not seek that. It did not lead the Bishop to lose his roots as a Nicaraguan, and his Dignity as a Nicaraguan.

The Bishop was very upset with Zelaya, as were a good part of the clergy, because the Liberal Revolution had a violent difference with the clergy, and above all with the Nicaraguan bishops. Zelaya sent him here and there, but the bishop never said: "I am going to ask the Yankees to come and invade Nicaragua", to oust Zelaya.

He was of the opinion, and he said so, he wrote so, as is stated in this Letter, that the problems of Nicaragua, and he was not speaking only of Nicaragua, but also of the Latin American peoples, we should solve the problems ourselves and the United States should not be interfering, intervening in the way they did, invading in the way they did, overthrowing governments in the way they did and as they continue to do.

I am going to read, what is a long Letter, one that he wrote in a difficult situation, with the Country occupied by Yankee troops, Yankee troops causing destruction, in collusion with the traitors. And what was his main resort? Well, surely he resorted every day asking Christ, asking God for the Yankee troops to leave. But he looked for a way to get through to the United States, because it was clear to him that the root of the problem was in American politics, nurtured by local traitors and quislings.

So he sends this Letter to a Reverend, a Cardinal, to His Eminence Cardinal James Gibbons, Archbishop of Baltimore, United States of America; that is, he addresses the Catholic Archbishop in Baltimore, who is a Cardinal, and tells him that he wants him to take his message to Congress, to the media in the United States, so that these abuses cease, that is all he has left as a remedy.

So he sends the Cardinal the letter. It is a long letter, I am going to read some paragraphs. He was writing to him from the Episcopal Palace of León, Nicaragua, Central America, in the year 1912, remember. He begins by saying:

"Most Eminent Sir and Most Dear Brother:

For many days misgivings have kept us from writing to you to implore your benevolence such that you may take an interest in the matters that we will present to your higher feelings..."

So he is appealing to someone who can make the North American clergy aware, who can make the North American congress people aware, and the North American government.

And he says: "Regrettable errors have put our Homeland, Nicaragua, under special circumstances that deprive it of much of of its autonomy, putting it at the discretion of foreign control".

What are the regrettable mistakes? The same as those Nicaraguans who invited Walker and Byron Cole, one of the political parties; there were Democrats and Legitimists, Conservatives and Liberals; at that time. Well, those who brought in Walker were the Liberals who were in their conflict with the Conservatives. And then in the case of Zeledón, back then in the time the Bishop was living, who brings the Yankee troops? The Conservatives, see for yourselves!

That is why he says: "Regrettable errors have placed our Homeland, Nicaragua, under special circumstances that deprive it of much of of its autonomy, putting it at the discretion of foreign control.

“And your great People, dearest brother, has made our little People feel the weight of its millions and its men; and your strong Homeland has subjected our weak Homeland to the force of its battleships, its powerful cannon while the treasure of the bankers of the North are strengthened by the daily and annihilating extraction of our exhausted coffers, by means of burdensome loans, unjust treaties, and unequal contracts".

Said by a Catholic Bishop, mind, in the year 1912. A Catholic Bishop who had been persecuted in those days by Zelaya’s Government, in which Love of the Homeland, Love of the People, Love of Sovereignty, of Dignity, weigh more than the discomfort he feels, with good reason, because Zelaya had expelled him, sent him to Granada and later to San Juan, he went to various places; and he left the Country and then returned. But he did not go looking for the Yankees to come and intervene in Nicaragua.

At the end of the day, it was a problem between Nicaraguans, provoked by two political parties, Liberals and Conservatives, that’s how those two wars originated. Ah! but the Yankees intervened.... What did the Yankees have to do here!

And in another paragraph, the bishop’s letter says: "We therefore require your invaluable cooperation to ensure in an effective way that the conditions created in Nicaragua by its relations with your country cease". So he continues asking the Cardinal to speak with the high representatives of the US Congress so the atrocities and violence cease.

He adds: "Far from any political considerations we have always tried to address our differences at a higher level (speaking of the political differences being lived through or having been experienced at that moment), observing always, along with our priests, a strict neutrality; we know our duties as Prelate and Pastor and it has been our determination to be, today and always, a father to everyone without distinction of social class, political party or special interests; nothing has in the least way changed our standard of conduct, neither flattery, nor threats; those of us who have behaved in this way; who are conscious of what we are and what we should aspire to be, can well on this occasion pledge on our honor as priest and citizen, promising that, upon the liberation of Nicaragua from the intimidating presence of US  weapons, the most absolute Peace and strictest order will reign among their fellow citizens, being Nicaraguans, whatever calls may be made on the Government of the Republic, provided that the rights and guarantees of those governed are respected.”

He is telling them clearly that it is Nicaraguans who have to reach understanding with each other and seek that stability. And he begins to speak to them about intervention:

"Intervention. You cannot imagine, Most Eminent Sir, how hard this word is. You live in a powerful and free People. You have not suffered the deep breakdown that has lacerated the soul of our Brothers Nouel and Mercier!

You have not felt the double pain of this bishop and citizen on hearing the echo of steel-edged army boots resounding under the naves of your churches. You have not wept with pain to see the banner of the conquering power flying in the spires of your Cathedral while the crossed labarum of Christ is lowered...".

Because the Yankees raised their flags, everywhere they placed the US flag, wherever there were high places it was there the Yankees placed their Flag.

"You have never seen on the ground where you were born, some foreign banner rise high above your own country’s flag; never has the venerated land where the ashes of your dead rest been trodden under foot… Because here they disrespected even the Cemeteries, disturbing in them the blessed Peace of the sepulchres; you have not seen a sanctuary turned into barracks".

They even converted the sanctuaries into barracks, the Yankee troops. We have also seen, in recent times, how the coup plotters turned the sanctuaries into barracks. So you can see, it's no accident.

"You have not seen the sanctuary turned into a barracks and the altar where the Eucharistic bread is distributed turned into a mess table for a gang of soldiers!”

In just the same way as the 2018 coup plotters took the churches and turned them into barracks, where there were weapons, bombs, and where they disguised themselves, and where they kidnapped and tortured people in those churches.

He continues to speak to the Cardinal, saying, with much tact :

"These considerations, Most Eminent Brother, may excuse my phrases if they upset your national feeling; forgive us: We speak to you from the heart, with all its vehemence, with all its pain, with all sincerity; we speak to you our brother without our language being one of sanitized formalism, on the contrary, we open up our heart, showing you our wounds in the intimate confidence of finding in you the remedy it needs".

In other words, he is speaking in Truth, the same Truth for which Christ died.

"For all these reasons, we still believe that it is advantageous to reach an understanding between our Homeland and the American Nation.”

And we too hold that position; when the Revolution triumphed we said: We want to have good relations with the United States. After the Revolution was victorious in '79, that September we went to Washington. We went to the White House and we met there with President Carter, because we wanted good relations with the United States, respect, good relations based respect, because when they are based on submission they are no longer good relations.

Look carefully at that.

"For all these reasons, we still believe that it is advantageous to reach an understanding.”

We make these words of the Bishop our own right now, and we say so to the United States, we say so to its government.

“For all that, in spite of all that has happened, of all the damage caused, we still believe it is advantageous that there should be an understanding between our Homeland and the American Nation; but that this should always be on the basis of equity and mutual interests; that it should not affect in anything our religion, our freedom, our autonomy, our language; nor try to deprecate our race, noble and audacious by our Iberian ancestry, irreducible and vigorous by our indigenous heritage, capable of every effort, every heroism and whose record in the glorious history of America, fills it with prestige assigning it, for its Past, a preferential place among the first civilizations and reserving for it, in years to come, an enviable and brilliant Future".

The Sun that doesn’t set! The Sun that doesn’t set! He is seeing that: the Sun that doesn’t set! A Bishop is saying it, take note, an exceptional Bishop! And his words are valid.

"I will not finish, Eminent Brother, my already long letter, without showing you that the feeling of suspicion and distrust on the part of the American Nations towards the United States... “

When he speaks of the American Nations he is speaking of all America and of the distrust that in those years already, 1912, the American nations have of the United States. He is saying it very clearly.

"...without showing you that the feeling of suspicion and distrust on the part of the American nations towards the United States is due more than anything else to the calculated and opportunist policy it has sometimes applied among our weak political nationalities, when it has almost always been among the main factors necessary for a sincere understanding: the diplomatic and consular agents of the United States, have not on all occasions been strict servants of their Government, but rather allies behaving in a way inappropriate to their posts.
