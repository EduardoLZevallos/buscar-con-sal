Mamdani vs Vance - A fixed prizefight to usher in a 2027 Blue/Red civil war

Imagine a fractured America in 2027, where the federal government squares off against defiant blue states in a prizefight that makes the infamous fixed fight between Raging Bull Jake LaMotta vs. Billy Fox look authentic. Just like in 1947, the mob wins when the odds are artificially stacked in its favor and every poor fool is naively betting on a fair fight until it’s too late.

This isn’t just civil unrest—it’s a federal-state implosion, banked on the simmering chaos in flood-ravaged or incinerated cities like Maui, Los Angeles, Asheville, New Orleans, and Kerrville. Contrived environmental catastrophes, coupled with an exploding nationwide unhoused population (that is drugged, starved, and mentally and physically ill and without adequate healthcare)— spark the fireworks as governmental conflict and mismanagement become an excuse for its abject failure.

This sets the stage for a color revolution, orchestrated by a predator class—transnational elites unbound by borders or morality—who profit by shorting a tanked U.S. economy. In one corner stands Zohran Mamdani, the so-called socialist firebrand new mayor of New York City, backed by George Soros and Neville Roy Singham’s millions. In the other is JD Vance, the Thiel-groomed president, who ascends after Trump’s orchestrated health collapse.

Peter Thiel. [Source: sfchronicle.com]

War games and real-time data point to a perfect storm: food shortages, economic collapse, environmental disasters, and a federal-state rift that fuels public outrage because it is incompetent in the delivery of necessary services but a wizard at surveillance. This is the predator class’s endgame—a techno-totalitarian takeover masked as chaos that necessitates the rollout of digital IDs and banking and social credit scores. This has already happened in China and is ramping up in Brazil with the 2020 rollout of Caixa Tem, a mobile banking app created by Caixa Econômica Federal, one of Brazil’s largest government-owned banks, to distribute emergency social benefits during the COVID-19 pandemic.

The Players: Mamdani’s Revolt vs. Vance’s Regime

Consider that on November 5, 2025: Zohran Mamdani, a 33-year-old Ugandan-born woke star, brought to us by the Democratic Socialists of America, an organization which functions as controlled opposition for the Democratic Party, wins New York City’s mayoral race, riding a wave of 46% support in Quinnipiac polls, trouncing Andrew Cuomo (33%) and Curtis Sliwa (15%).

Betting markets give him a 93% shot, fueled by TikTok charisma and empty promises of rent caps and dog-and-pony Palestinian solidarity parades. These are the only two issues the so-called socialist left is permitted to rally against—besides its members giving lip service to identity issues that in effect run roughshod over women and girls’ rights and fracture a united working-class movement. This “left” is not permitted to develop an integrated anti-imperialist, anti-war movement that transcends racial, sexual, and party lines or defend personal sovereignty.

While pretending otherwise, Singham aligns CodePink with U.S. foreign policy in its positions toward sanctioned states targeted by the State Department for regime change. The group has publicly supported U.S. and international sanctions against Myanmar’s military government—a stance directly aligned with the U.S. State Department’s policy framework. It has also foregrounded women’s rights in Iran as a central advocacy theme, paralleling the State Department’s use of that issue as a soft-power wedge. Yet, CodePink remains notably silent on Saudi Arabia’s systemic violations of women’s rights, despite extensive documentation by human-rights organizations—again mirroring official U.S. diplomatic restraint toward Riyadh.

Mamdani’s war chest is a Soros-Singham juggernaut: Open Society Foundations funneled $37 million to the Working Families Party (2020-2024), $5.15 million to its 501(c)(4), and $34.4 million via Tides, while Singham’s network—through groups like Code Pink and “Jews for Zohran”—pumped $10 million into anti-Israel ads and grassroots donors [InfluenceWatch] [NYC CFB].

Neville Roy Singham [Source: wikibi.in]

NYC, with its $2.6 trillion gross metropolitan product, becomes a woke fortress: defunded police, rent freezes, and City Hall draped in globalist flags. However, when it comes to actual workers’ rights involving bodily autonomy, he is nowhere to be found. Singham’s People’s Forum required vaccine passports long after NYC reversed its policy [Archive]. And all Soros and Singham’s groups forbid any talk that questioned the forcing of unconstitutional healthcare mandates.

George Soros [Source: foxnews.com]

While NYC private-sector employee vaccine mandate ended (i.e., became optional) on November 1, 2022 and the NYC municipal employee vaccine mandate was rescinded on February 10, 2023, The People’s Forum continues to advertise events requiring “proof of vaccination.” Their recurring event page for Red Hot Summer: SWANA Beats lists dates through November 2025 and still states, “Proof of vaccination is required for all in-person events.” Likewise, a 2025 book-talk event listing carries the notice, “If attending in person, please be prepared to show proof of vaccination and ID at the door.” Earlier archived pages from late 2021 through early 2023 show the same requirement, indicating that The People’s Forum maintained a vaccine-passport policy for more than two years beyond NYC’s rollback.

Even more telling is that according to a July 2025 article, Zohran Mamdani is reported to align with Eric Adams’ position of refusing to re-hire city workers terminated under the vaccine mandate. “This position comes years after thousands of city employees were dismissed for non-compliance with the mandate.”

In Washington, the script flips. Donald Trump, 79, succumbs to health woes—chronic venous insufficiency, visible hand bruises, and swollen ankles from endless campaign trails, reported as early as July 2025. A September 2025 YouGov poll shows 66% of Americans doubting his governance capacity, and whispers of a 25th Amendment move or quiet resignation grow louder.

Enter JD Vance, the Thiel-anointed VP, elevated to president. Backed by Peter Thiel’s $15 million Senate seed and $50 million from donors like Elon Musk ($45 million/month to America PAC), David Sacks ($1 million), and Palmer Luckey ($1.5 million) [OpenSecrets], Vance is the predator class’s new avatar. His agenda: mass deportations, crypto deregulation, and Palantir’s CIA-honed surveillance ($113 million in federal contracts) locking down the heartland.

The clash is inevitable. Vance brands Mamdani a “socialist radical” on X, while Mamdani’s TikTok army dubs Vance “Thiel’s puppet.” NYC’s ports and Wall Street become battlegrounds, with blue-state allies—Los Angeles, Chicago, Seattle—defying federal edicts, while red states enforce blockades. It’s city versus fed, with America’s fracture as the prize.

The Powder Keg: Triggers of Chaos

War games from RAND and DHS, leaked via FOIA, pinpoint the catalysts: food shortages, economic collapse, environmental disasters, and plagues. The IMF’s 40% recession risk for 2026 hits early—U.S. debt balloons to $38 trillion [Treasury], tariffs choke trade, and USDA droughts devastate crops. Empty shelves spark riots in Chicago, where Antifa and Proud Boys, suspected CIA proxies, reignite clashes costing $23 million in federal response, as seen in Portland 2020.

In Louisiana, FEMA’s botched flood relief—underfunded by Vance’s austerity—births Cajun militias, echoing X threads about “government betrayal.” A lab-leak “plague 2.0,” reminiscent of Debbie Lerman’s DOD-driven COVID exposé, quarantines blue cities, with Vance blaming Mamdani’s “open borders.”

Federal-state divides pour gasoline on the fire. Blue states, led by Mamdani’s NYC, invoke the 10th Amendment, withholding taxes and declaring autonomy. Red-state attorneys general counter with treason lawsuits, while militias—Midwest farmers, urban gangs—arm up. X buzzes with scenarios: “NYC vs. DC, 2027 civil war.” Hollywood’s predictive programming—Civil War, Bushwick, Obama’s Leave the World Behind—has primed the public for urban hellscapes and red-blue sieges.

The Predator Class’s Endgame: Color Revolution and Techno-Totalitarianism

This isn’t chaos for chaos’s sake—it’s a color revolution, Soros-style, with a Thiel twist. Soros’s playbook—tanking economies via protests and currency bets (think 1992’s Black Wednesday)—is legendary. His $37 million to Mamdani’s allies fuels street optics: BLM, Free Palestine, No Kings Day.

Thiel’s techno-totalitarianism counters with Palantir’s surveillance grid and Vance’s deportation machine, consolidating power for a BlackRock-led panopticon. Both sides short the collapsing economy—$14 billion in corporate cash already rigged 2020’s election, and 2027’s chaos is their jackpot.

January 6 was the rehearsal: a “staged insurrection” with 26 FBI informants, four entering the Capitol [DOJ IG], and ~274 plainclothes agents shaping the narrative. Now, the predator class scales up, pitting Mamdani’s urban revolt against Vance’s federal crackdown. The goal? A fractured America where elites control the ashes—digital IDs, centralized crypto, and AI-driven compliance, tested during COVID’s 3 million excess deaths.

Beneath the red-blue theater, however, lies the predator class’s true consensus: a unified push for digital IDs and banking infrastructure, sold as a lifeline amid the engineered crisis. Soros’s Open Society Foundations, through grants advancing public health and equity, have long championed digital tools for “access to services,” funding initiatives that integrate biometric verification for aid distribution and refugee identification—framed as empowering the marginalized but laying the groundwork for universal tracking. Singham, the Maoist tech mogul now ensconced in Shanghai, echoes this via his network of pro-CCP media and consultancies, promoting data-driven governance to “eradicate poverty,” mirroring China’s own systems where digital wallets enforce compliance under the guise of welfare.

Their position: In the chaos of fracture, digital IDs will “help people get the life-saving services they need,” from emergency rations in riot-torn cities to subsidized housing in Mamdani’s rent-capped enclaves, all while quietly compiling dossiers on every transaction and movement.

On the opposing flank, Thiel and Musk align seamlessly, their libertarian veneer masking the same surveillance imperative. Thiel’s Palantir, seeded by CIA’s In-Q-Tel, has already embedded AI-driven identity platforms in federal agencies, enabling seamless data fusion for “national security”—a blueprint for mandatory digital verification in Vance’s deportation sweeps and economic blockades. Musk’s X Money, partnering with Visa for peer-to-peer digital wallets launching in 2025, positions itself as the “everything app” for instant aid transfers during blackouts and shortages, with Musk touting it as a way to “bypass broken banks” and deliver “real-time relief.”

Their shared stance: Digital banking and IDs are essential to “streamline services” in a collapsing economy, allowing citizens to access federal stipends or crypto relief funds without cash—while Palantir’s algorithms score users in the background, flagging “disloyal” behaviors for Vance’s heartland enforcers.

This bipartisan elite accord on digital infrastructure isn’t coincidence; it’s the predator class’s borderless masterstroke. Unfettered by nation-states, they envision a seamless rollout where Soros-Singham’s “equity portals” in blue havens sync with Thiel-Musk’s “efficiency networks” in red strongholds, creating a national panopticon. The pitch remains humanitarian: “Life-saving services” via app-linked IDs for food drops in Chicago sieges or medical quarantines in Louisiana floods.

Yet the fine print is dystopian—ushering in social credit scores that lock down physical movement, turning America into open-air prisons. Dissenters—be they Mamdani’s protesters or Vance’s militias—face algorithmic exile: no loans for “untrustworthy” farmers, no gigs for “radical” creators on X. Beijing’s playbook, refined under Xi, proves the template: 17 million blacklisted by 2025 for “disruptive” acts [Social Credit System], barred from trains, planes, and high-speed jobs, their finances throttled to enforce silence.

As 2027 looms, with Mamdani’s Gotham defying Vance’s empire, the choice is stark: unite or let the predator class turn America into another Hollywood dystopian tale. Data from FEC, OpenSecrets, NYC CFB, and IRS 990s confirm the money trails; Politico and NYT track the political chessboard.
