APRIL 18, 2023: REMEMBERING THE 2018 ATTEMPTED US COUP AGAINST NICARAGUA

1. Upcoming Events

April 23, 2023:  Sustainable Agriculture and Environment in  Nicaragua (register here) Nicaragua emits less than one eighth as much greenhouse gas per capita as the US. And yet Nicaraguans:  Produce 90% of their own food. Have eliminated most hunger and illiteracy. Have addressed income disparities. Continue improving production efficiency. Have democratic control of food production. Have nearly achieved economic parity among women and men. How did Nicaragua do it? In this webinar, you will hear what we learned.

April 28-29: Burying 200 Years of the Monroe Doctrine  Program: Opposing Sanctions and Unilateral Intervention; Countering Militarism with Peace and Cooperation; Unearthing the Root Causes of Immigration; Forging an Independent Economic Model

2. Remembering the 2018 Attempted US Coup 

Dan Kovalik, John Perry: Five Years Ago in Nicaragua: A Coup Attempt Begins Five years ago, Nicaragua was subject to a violent attempted US coup that lasted from April through July, 2018. In the first of four articles, we look at how it was planned and how it started. Nicaragua’s relatively loose regulation of local nonprofits at that time enabled the US to pour as much as $200 million into opposition media, NGOs and “human rights” bodies. By April 19, the scene was set for greater violence as “students” suddenly had access to hundreds of homemade mortar guns, deployed at roadblocks made by ripping up paving stones. On that day, the first of 22 police officers was killed.

A Timeline of Events in Nicaragua, April-July 2018, the time of the US failed attempt to overthrow Nicaragua’s government People in the US were mislead by corporate media propaganda about the violence during this period, which was exclusively blamed on the Sandinista government. This presents what actually happened.
