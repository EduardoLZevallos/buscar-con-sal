Office Move

On 27 March, we are moving from our current office in Durham Road to new premises! We will no longer have an office but are moving into a co-working space where we will have a dedicated desk, some storage space and access to meeting rooms. This has been partly brought about due to a move to more remote working by our part-time staff, but more importantly as a cost-saving measure given that our resources are really stretched. Our new address will be as follows: - NSCAG, c/o Space 4, 113-115 Fonthill Road, London N4 3HH.

NEU Conference

We are delighted to announce that we have a Nicaraguan guest with us this year at the NEU Conference. She is Aleyda Cruz Flores, a primary school teacher who is a member of Nicaraguan teachers’ union ANDEN. We have organised a fringe meeting on Education in Nicaragua during the Conference. Aleyda will be speaking and will be joined by Guisell Morales, Nicaraguan Ambassador to the UK and Ireland and Heather Marcano, a participant in the NEU-sponsored teacher exchange with Nicaragua which took place last year. The fringe meeting will take place during lunchtime on Wednesday 5 April – full details will be available from the NSCAG stall during the Conference, so we hope that NEU members attending the Conference will come and see us!

Nicaraguan prisoner release

Maternity Waiting Homes

Since 2013, the Nicaraguan government has promoted a strategy of building maternity waiting homes in order to reduce perinatal maternal mortality. Currently there are 181 maternity waiting homes to assist women in the final period of their pregnancy. The women are mainly from rural areas, although they can also come from the urban sector if they wish. The mayor of Ticuantepe, Dr. Ligia Ramirez, said that the strategy is to have at least one maternity waiting home in each municipality. “It is a strategy that has supported the family, because there are people who live in places that are not close to health centres and here they can be seen by doctors. They can sleep and they will have their baby in a hospital or a well-staffed health centre. Here they come to rest, to relax, to de-stress and also to receive timely medical attention. This strategy has led to a decrease in maternal mortality. The women can come here two weeks before [their due dates] and sometimes they can stay for more than a month, depending on their condition”. Among the benefits they receive are lodging, food, and education on subjects such as dental care, self-esteem, women’s rights, risks during pregnancy, and newborn care. The maternity waiting homes have a staff dedicated to the care of pregnant women who provide 24-hour care.

Nicaragua Launches Booklet on Rights of Persons with Disabilities

Nicaraguan government ministries have launched a booklet entitled “Attention and care for persons with disabilities” to promote and guarantee the full exercise of their human rights. The booklet states that every person with a disability has the right to be part of education, health, sports, culture, work, and political activities. The booklet guides the family on how to care for and attend in a loving and respectful way people with disabilities who require greater support to live well, with peace of mind, wellbeing and a better quality of life. Through the programme Todos con Voz (everyone deserves a voice) house to house visits are made as well as delivery of aid to individuals and families. The head of the Ministry of the Family, Johana Flores, said, “It is one more effort that adds to this whole strategy of promoting life…. We are mandated to work with the family and the community to strengthen the restoration of the rights of our brothers and sisters with disabilities.”

Tripartite Commission Agrees on 10% Salary Increase

On 23 February, Minister of Labour Alba Luz Torres, trade union leaders, and private sector representatives agreed on a 10% percent increase in the minimum wage for ten economic sectors effective from 1st March. The leader of teachers’ union ANDEN, Professor José Antonio Zepeda, emphasised that this agreement will allow workers to recover their purchasing power. “Obviously, for us as a union movement, this is always about prioritising employment. This agreement follows that principle of tripartism, the principle through which our government establishes the active and direct participation of workers in the decision-making process.”
