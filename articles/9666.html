Nicaraguan opposition NGOs and media on the US government payroll

Submitted bytortilla onMié, 08/07/2020 - 09:22

Giorgio Trucchi, 8 July 2020
https://kaosenlared.net/nicaragua-opositores-en-nomina-de-eeuu-video/

Last Friday (3/7) the program "Sin Fronteras", directed by journalist William Grigsby Vado, presented evidence of something that is already well known: The United States, through government agencies and related private organizations, has been financing - as it continues to do - Nicaraguan opposition groups. Between 2017 and 2018, almost U$28 million was disbursed. Especially in 2018, the main organizations that promoted and/or supported the failed coup attempt received $16 million.

Among the biggest beneficiaries were Grupo CINCO (Carlos Fernando Chamorro /Sofia Montenegro | Confidencial) with U$ 3.3 million, the Institute of Strategic Studies and Public Policy IEEPP (Felix Maradiaga) with U$ 2.1 million, Comisión Permanente de Derechos Humanos CPDH (Marcos Carmona) with U$ 2 million, Centro Nicaragüense de Derechos Humanos CENIDH (Vilma Núñez) with U$ 1 million and Grupo Ética y Transparencia (Roberto Courtney) also with U$ 1 million.

On the USAID payroll are also the Movement for Nicaragua MpN (Violeta Granera) with U$ 803 thousand, Let's Make Democracy (Luciano García) U$ 800 thousand, Violeta Barrios de Chamorro Foundation (Cristiana Chamorro) U$ 600 thousand, a supposed Peasant Movement (Medardo Mairena? ) U$ 240 thousand, the executive direction of the Civic Alliance for Justice and Democracy U$ 212 thousand, Popol Na Foundation (Mónica López Baltodano) U$ 208 thousand, Nicaraguan Association for Human Rights ANPDH (Álvaro Leiva) U$ 200 thousand and the Autonomous Women's Movement MAM with U$ 154 thousand.

Communication, electoral observation and human rights received 75% of the funds (US$ 12 million).
 
These are only the funds received from USAID and some other US agencies. To these should be added those that opposition organizations - including a wide range of so-called "independent media" - received from other organizations (Open Society Foundation for example), as well as from European governments and agencies.
