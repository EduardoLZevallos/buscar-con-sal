DANIEL : "Our flag is respected! And anyone who comes here to disrespect our people, our Patriotic Nation, well, let them not appear again in Nicaragua. We don't want relations with that interventionist government!"

Enviado por tortilla el Dom, 02/10/2022 - 18:00

Opening address for the Police Parade : “With the Strength of Victories”

On the 43rd Anniversary of the National Police

September 30th 2022

 

Address by Daniel

 

Nicaraguan brothers and sisters; families of this land of Heroes and Martyrs; of working men and women, of the city and the countryside; of a Youth growing in Education and with Education, from early childhood they are developing, childhood with education. And schools are multiplying throughout our country, for all ages.

 

Schools are multiplying throughout our country, so that our Youth who for centuries have been marginalized, young people who come from peasant families, are incorporated not only into Primary Education, or Secondary Education, but have access in these times to Technical Careers, Science, Technology, Art, which are the heritage of Humanity and therefore are the heritage of our People.

 

Beloved brothers and sisters of the National Police; beloved families of the National Police, a Police force that has been forged over 43 years. One that has been forging itself, preparing, training, to serve the People, to serve the Citizens. Preparing and training also to defend the Right to Peace of all Nicaraguan families, without exception. To defend the Right to Work of all Nicaraguan families, without exception. To defend the Right to Health, without exception; the Right to Health for all Nicaraguan families.

 

The right to Health is also multiplying, thanks to the efforts of the People-President, and with the unconditional cooperation of brotherly Peoples and institutions that are created to bring aid, cooperation to developing peoples, we have managed to install hospitals, health posts and maternal shelters, throughout the country.

 

First-class hospitals, where patients who come from Health Posts, from Health Centers, who need medical care of a higher level, which now requires technology, medical instruments, specialists in the Hospitals that we have in all regions of our Country, patients are received there.

 

And we continue to open hospitals, even when occasionally some wretched individual may spring out ... the human wretchedness of it! A European country, the Netherlands, a few years ago they committed to building, not one of the big modern hospitals that we are building, but a small clinic, a small hospital in a community on the Caribbean Coast of Nicaragua, where the Original Peoples, the Miskitos, the Mayagnas, the Ramaquí, the Afrodescendants are.

 

Well, the ambassador of that country came to visit, our Foreign Minister received her yesterday, and what did they hear? The ambassador came to lecture Nicaraguans, as if Nicaragua was a Dutch colony, like one of those they still have in some regions and those that they held and multiplied back when the colonies held by European governments proliferated, colonies of the ruling classes in those European countries.

 

They became owners! They became owners and set out to exploit, just as they exploited Our America, they also exploited Africa, Asia. There was no region of the World that was not exploited, dominated, in what is a crime that still stands, a crime that cannot be forgotten, and now some Governments appear to believe this is still the colonial era and that our Peoples and our Governments are still Peoples and Governments submissive to foreign Crowns. Because the governments in question have, many of them, Kings, or Queens.

 

And after launching that outrageous speech against the dignity of our People, she communicated that they were not going to build their famous hospital, that they were not going to build it. A hospital they had been committed to building for several years, but they had not laid a single stone, not a single brick, and all she had to say were threats, threats and more threats.

 

And what have we said to this lady ambassador, and what do we say to that Government, to those Ladies and Gentlemen? Here, in this Land, in this Patriotic Nation, our Flag is respected, the Blue and White Flag that our General Sandino defended with the Red and Black Flag... Our flag is respected! And anyone who comes here to disrespect our people, our Patriotic Nation, well, let them not appear again in Nicaragua. We don't want relations with that interventionist government!

 

Once outside, as the song says so well: Out there they can shout whatever they want, all their miseries they will shout out, but here in Nicaragua, on this Land, we do not allow them to disrespect this People.

 

We also say the same to the US Senators, who are important Senators, because from the US Senate, from the US Congress, all the interventions that Yankee imperialism has carried out in the world have been endorsed and approved. That is, there they feel like owners of the World and that they can just gobble up its Peoples.

 

Now it turns out that they proposed an Ambassador for Nicaragua, we gave them the ok when the Placet request proposing the Ambassador arrived. Then the candidate for ambassador to Nicaragua appeared before the Senate, and what took place in the Senate? Insult, disrespect, and talk as if William Walker was coming here to Nicaragua again, to take over Nicaragua. That was how he spoke before the Senate. Then we immediately said: Get out! Get out! He's staying out! And out there, let him keep shouting whatever he wants, but here in Nicaragua, our Flag is respected!

 

Compañera Rosario, Vice President of the Republic; First Commissioner General Francisco Díaz, Director General of the National Police; Army General Julio César Avilés Castillo, Commander-in-Chief of the Glorious Army of Nicaragua; Commissioner General Ramón Antonio Avellán Medal, Deputy Director General of the National Police; Commissioner General Adolfo Joel Marenco Corea, Deputy Director General of the National Police; and Compañera Amelia Coronel Kinloch, Minister of the Interior.

 

Compañero Gustavo Porras, President of the National Assembly; Compañera Brenda Isabel Rocha Chacón, National Hero, Presiding Magistrate of the Supreme Electoral Council of Nicaragua; Compañera Alba Luz Ramos Vanegas, Presiding Magistrate of the Supreme Court of Justice.

 

Major General Bayardo Ramón Rodríguez Ruiz, Chief of the General Staff of the Nicaraguan Army; Major General Marvin Elías Corrales Rodríguez, Inspector General of the Nicaraguan Army; Commissioner General Aldo Sáenz Ulloa, Deputy Director General of the National Police; Commissioner General Jaime Vanegas Vega, Inspector General of the National Police.

 

Compañero Denis Moncada Colindres, Minister of Foreign Affairs of Nicaragua; Compañero Laureano Ortega Murillo, Advisor for Investments, Trade and International Cooperation of the Presidency of the Republic; Compañera Reyna Juanita Rueda Alvarado, Mayor of Managua; Compañera Wendy Carolina Morales Urbina, Attorney General of the Republic of Nicaragua; Compañera Darling Carolina Ríos Munguía, Procurator for the Defense of Human Rights; Compañera Nahima Díaz, Director of TELCOR.

 

Compañero Luis Ángel Montenegro, Superintendent of Banks and other Financial Institutions; Compañera Ramona Rodríguez Pérez, President of the National Council of Universities; Doctor Maribel Duriez González, President of the Evaluation and Accreditation Council.

 

Compañero@s, Guerrilla and Brigade Commander René Vivas Lugo, former Director of the National Police; Regimental Commander Doris Tijerino Haslam.

 

And too we greet all the Compañeras and Compañeros of the Cabinet, of the People President, of the Government, because we could spend the whole night here greeting them one by one. For all of you, beloved brothers and sisters, the embrace of the Nicaraguan People.

 

Well, the rain accompanies us, as it accompanied the students, now it accompanies the Police, and accompanies the People, the rain accompanies Nicaragua. Blessed rain for the benefits it brings, but we know very well that we have to take care of ourselves, we have to protect ourselves when the rain falls hard and tragedies occur.

 

Beloved Ambassador of the People's Republic of China, Ambassador Chen Xi... Welcome beloved Ambassador! beloved Ambassador of Russia Alexander Khokholikov; beloved Colonel Alexander Gurtovoy, Military Attaché of Russia; beloved Colonel Oleg Surov, Head of the Training Center of the Ministry of Internal Affairs of Russia; Compañero Oleg Alexandrovich Vorobev, First Secretary of the Russian Embassy.


Beloved brother Ambassador of Mexico, Guillermo Zamora Villa... Welcome, brother! And greetings to your beloved wife, Valentina Toussaint. Long live Mexico!

 

Ambassador of Cuba, welcome beloved brother, Jorge Luis Mayo Fernández; beloved Compañera Débora Quintana, wife of the Ambassador of Cuba; beloved Compañero Lieutenant Colonel Ernesto Martínez, Military, Naval and Air Attaché of the Republic of Cuba.

 

Beloved brother Ambassador of the Bolivarian Republic of Venezuela, Francisco Arrúe. The land of Chávez, the land of Bolívar, the land of Nicolás Maduro, a Heroic People, always giving battle. Mr. Anzonit Albano, First Secretary of the Embassy of Venezuela.

 

Beloved Ambassador of the sister Republic of Colombia, Leon Freddy Muñoz Lopera... Welcome, brother! Minister Counselor Francisco Niño Contreras, of the sister Republic of Colombia.

beloved brother Majid Salehi, Ambassador of the Republic of Iran. Beloved brother Ambassador of Abkhazia, Inar Ladariya, and Mrs. Valeria Logua, First Secretary. beloved brother Ambassador of Argentina, Mateo Daniel Capitanich.

 

Beloved brother Ambassador of the Republic of El Salvador, Hugo Javier Merino Lazo. beloved Minister Counselor, Israel Landaverde; beloved Salvadoran Brother, Defense Attaché, Colonel Jorge Alberto Menjivar, and beloved Salvadoran Brother, Police Attaché, Commissioner José Luis Mancia Valle.

 

Beloved brother Ambassador of the Sahrawi Democratic Embassy, Uali Ali Salem. Beloved brother Diego Ariel Rodríguez Camacho, Chargé d'affaires of the Republic of Bolivia. Beloved Central American Brother, Charge d'affaires of the Guatemalan Embassy, Pedro Emilio Lechuga. beloved Central American sister, from the land of Morazán, Marlene Andrade, Chargé d'affaires of the Embassy of Honduras; beloved brother, Colonel Román Gómez Reyes, Attaché of Military, Naval and Air Defense. Beloved State Councilor of Japan, Kawashima Hiroaki... Welcome!

 

How many Peoples are represented by these Ambassadors! And there is a cry in all the peoples: Peace! And there is also a Heroic Struggle among these Peoples against the blockade that has been going on for more than 60 years against the sister Republic of Cuba. The Cuba of Martí, the Cuba of Fidel, the Cuba of Raúl, the Cuba of our brother President, who is waging this gigantic Battle against the blockade.

 

There has never been a blockade as brutal as the one in place against Cuba, and now our brother Miguel Díaz-Canel is at the forefront, during truly terrible moments, where the blockade has intensified, where difficult situations have occurred, including with this hurricane that has just passed. The country is blockaded, imagine, and a hurricane with devastating power passes causing destruction, death and affecting the entire population.

 

Why? Because even the electrical power system, the generating stations were shaken, affected by the hurricane. But this is a Heroic People, a People that has been able to withstand a thousand battles and still emerged victorious. And we are sure, beloved brothers and sisters, that Cuba will emerge victorious again, with the Solidarity of the Peoples of the World, in the face of these battles.

 

These are the moments in which the cruelty, the brutality of imperialism is exposed; these are the moments in which, if there were even a little humanity left in the United States Government, in the US State, there may be a little Humanity left, because they should lift the blockade.

 

These are extreme circumstances that demand the lifting of the blockade, the United Nations should be demanding this, where year after year the vast majority of countries propose the end of the blockade. And this is the moment, at a time when Cuba is suffering like no one else, like no other People, when the blockade on the fraternal Cuban People must be lifted.

 

And if the blockade is lifted, then we will be able to believe that there is still a little Humanity left in the United States of America. We know that there is Humanity in the American People, in Solidarity with our Struggles and in Solidarity with the Cuban People, but we know that there is no Humanity in the American ruling class, who one by one have been tightening the screw, trying to drown, to murder, to force the People of Cuba to surrender. But the People of Cuba have shown that they are a People who do not sell themselves out, nor will they ever sell themselves out !

 

Long live Cuba, and long live that Heroic People !

 

Now, sisters and brothers, let's give way to the Parade of our brother and sister Policemen, who after all, it is the People who recognize the value of the Police, the work of the Police, the efficiency of the Police. And naturally the police will have to develop even more, with greater strength and efficiency, with more capabilities, why? To continue combating any and every threat against Nicaragua's families.

 

Long live the National Police

on its 43rd Anniversary!
