NSCAG APRIL UPDATE

Webinar announcement

In this webinar, 12 students and faculty members of the University of Maryland in the USA will explain what they learned during a 2-week study abroad course with the Rural Workers Association, ATC. The webinar will take place on 23 April at 8pm UK time. They learned that Nicaragua emits less than one eighth as much greenhouse gas per capita as the US. And yet Nicaraguans produce 90% of their own food, have eliminated most hunger and illiteracy, have addressed income disparities, continue improving production efficiency, have democratic control of food production and have nearly achieved economic parity among women and men. How did Nicaragua do it? Join this webinar to hear what the students learned. To register go to this link.

Anniversary of Attempted Coup 2018

This month marks five years since the United States orchestrated and financed a violent coup attempt against the democratically elected FSLN government in Nicaragua. To mark the anniversary of the attempted coup, during which ordinary people were subject to terror attacks for sympathizing with their government and for supporting the Sandinista Revolution, the Monthly Review has agreed to publish a series of four articles by Dan Kovalik and John Perry which describe in detail what occurred during the coup attempt. The first in the series can be found here. The next in the series will be included in our May update.

See also article by Stephen Sefton – ‘Nicaragua 2018 – the Triumph of a Sovereign Future’ – link here and an article written by Francisco Dominguez in September 2021 – link here

Human Rights

While the US pays little regard to the human rights of many of its own citizens, it manifests intense interest in those of countries that it regards as its enemies. Nicaragua, designated by both Trump and Biden as a ‘strategic threat’ is seen as one of those enemies. The UN Human Rights Council recently released a new report focusing almost entirely on the human rights of the perpetrators of what became a violent coup attempt, rather than on the rights of the huge numbers of ordinary Nicaraguans who suffered the consequences of their violence. The report calls for additional sanctions in one of its recommendations. In doing so, it ignores the UN Human Rights Council’s own assessments of sanctions issued without its authority. John Perry has written on this subject in an article which explains how the UN is being used by the US in its propaganda war against Nicaragua – see link here

Trade Union Conferences

From 3-6 April, we attended the National Education Union Conference held in Harrogate. During the Conference, we held a successful fringe meeting on ‘Advancing the Education Revolution’ where speakers included invited guest Aleyda Cruz Flores, a Nicaraguan primary school teacher, Nicaraguan Ambassador Guisell Morales and two of the teachers - Heather Marcano and Anna Grainger – who participated in the NEU-sponsored Nicaraguan teacher exchange held in July/August last year. The meeting was chaired by Christine Blower, Labour Member of the House of Lords. Our thanks to the NEU for their hospitality and support.

We will be attending the GMB Congress taking place from 4-8 June in Brighton. We will have a stall at the Conference and hope that GMB members will come and say hello!
