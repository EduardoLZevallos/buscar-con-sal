7 things you should know before you go to Nicaragua

Submitted bytortilla onJue, 19/04/2018 - 18:16

Ellie Hattersley, Metro, April 17th 2018
http://metro.co.uk/2018/04/17/7-things-you-should-know-before-you-go-to…

Nicaragua is one of South America’s lesser-known highlights.

Easy to journey around, with a pretty well-established backpacker trail, it’s a great option for those who want to ease themselves into travel in this region.

It’s got heaps to keep you entertained: hikes up volcanoes and plentiful water sports on its many lakes, plus decent nightlife, Caribbean islands, and hidden surf spots for beginners and experts.

There are elegant colonial-style cities and beach towns a-plenty.

Calle La Calzada and Cathedral de Granada (Picture: Getty)

Here are seven things you need to know before you go.

It’s actually pretty safe

Central America has a reputation for being a bit dodgy.

However, for the most part, visits are trouble-free – and as long as you’re sensible, you’re unlikely to run into problems.

And Nicaragua was actually assessed by the World Economic Forum as being safer than the UK last year.

It has one of the oldest cities on the continent

Granada was founded in 1524 by the colonialist Francisco Hernandez de Cordoba, after whom the Nicaraguan currency was named.

And it’s beautiful.

And also plays host to the largest lake in Central America

Lake Nicaragua has an area of 8,264 square kilometres. It’s so large that the Spanish conquistadors mistook it for the ocean on first sight.

The island of Ometepe with its dual volcanoes perches prettily in the middle of the lake.

You’ll probably end up drinking a lot of rum

The award-winning Flor de Cana rum is the nation’s favourite drink and it’s gloriously cheap.

Expect to drink it often and probably in quantity.

And eating a lot of rice and beans

Nicaragua’s main staple is gallo pinto – rice and beans – and it is served anywhere and everywhere.

Often eaten at breakfast, you might find yourself a bit sick of the sight of it by the end of your trip.

The Corn islands are a clear highlight – but they’re a pain to get to

One of the main appeals of Nicaragua is its dual coasts: Pacific on the one side, Caribbean on the other.

The highlight of many trips is a trip to the Corn Islands on the Caribbean side.

However, it’s a bit of a trek whichever way you swing it.

The bus trip across the country from Managua takes about nine hours, then it’s a hairy six-hour boat ride from Bluefields. Or, you can opt for a $200 return flight to Big Corn.

Worth it, though.

You can board down a volcano

Certainly one of the most popular activities in the country is volcano boarding down the slopes of Cerro Negro.

Most people stay in Leon – well worth a visit in itself – and do the trip from there.

It’s epic.
