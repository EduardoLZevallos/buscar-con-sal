You can see, Nicaragua and Central America became independent from Spain in 1821, but, of course, more than 200 years of Spanish domination had already passed and some of the languages of our Original Peoples in whom we have our roots managed to survive, and with the Revolution we have given them more encouragement, more reinforcement, so that classes are given in Spanish and in the languages of the Miskito Community, Rama Key, and Higher Education Projects are also developed for them... That is, in communities that had been totally forgotten.

Then also we have something that is really extraordinary, we have built in record time a highway which for the first time, via a concrete road, links the Pacific Region with the Caribbean Region. That is a Miracle and that has only been possible because of the cooperation of Fraternal Nations, Fraternal Peoples, and thanks to the working capacity of the Institutions that are building these Roads, that are building Hospitals, that are building Schools, that are multiplying Housing everywhere. You can see this every day, every day you can see it.

As I was telling you, in 1821 came Independence, in 1821 we became independent and in 1855, imagine, the Yankees fell on us, William Walker invaded us, coming to take over the San Juan River and work with US companies on passenger traffic from the East Coast to the West Coast of the United States.

Because that was a period when there was a Gold Rush and everyone who was on the East Coast, where there was no Gold, when the news came to them that, on the West Coast, San Francisco, the area of Los Angeles, that there were large quantities of Gold there, that people were extracting Gold everywhere there; then thousands of Americans began to embark on that journey.

Cornelius Vanderbilt, a shipping entrepreneur, came up with the idea of putting into operation, and he did put it into operation, for passengers to leave from New York, and they arrived from New York to the coast of Nicaragua; since they were large ships that brought a lot of passengers, they had smaller ships there waiting and then they sailed across with those ships so as to arrive in the area of San Juan del Sur, where the strip of land is small.

There, the stagecoaches of the shipping company were waiting for them, they would get on, go the short distance in the stagecoaches, to where other big ships were waiting to take them to the West Coast where the Gold was. That became known as the Transit Route.

And take note that this caused conflicts to start in the United States among the shipping companies and they hired a group of Officers, Soldiers, Yankees, mercenaries, to support one of those companies. And the mercenaries came in the year 1855 and started fighting. And who was paying the mercenaries? The other shipping company. And the other company whom did they work with here in Nicaragua? There were two governments: one in León and one in Granada.

So, one of the governments had to be eliminated, and in the end, the head of the expedition, William Walker, was elected President of Nicaragua. How many people will know in the world that Nicaragua had a Yankee President recognized by the State Department? And that this Yankee President appointed a Priest as his Ambassador, and that the Priest went to present his Credentials to Washington, and they were received there.

But if you take a good look, some Nicaraguans sold out, but not all of them did, there were some who, faced with that outrage, what they did was arm themselves with rifles albeit of less capacity than those brought by the Yankees, which were of higher quality. These Nicaraguans got together with the Indian archers from Matagalpa, and they were the Nicaraguans, who neither surrendered nor sold out, and they put up a fight defeating the invading Yankee. I would say that this is the first defeat the invading Yankees had here in this entire Region.

But they continued to be obsessed with invading Nicaragua once, twice, overthrowing governments. And thus we have all our Heroes, in the Battle of San Jacinto the Hero who stands out most is Andrés Castro, when he lifted the stone and cast it at the Yankee, and knocked him down. That is to say, he was not afraid of the Yankee coming at him with a rifle, but he came, grabbed the stone, since he did not have a rifle, and cast the stone and knocked the Yankee down. And that Battle was decisive, led by General José Dolores Estrada.

Then came the other invasions, invasions by the English, the English wanted to take over Nicaragua too, as did the Germans, and they formed expeditions with famous Admirals, but they defeated them, they always defeated them.

The Nicaraguans who had already forged themselves through the mixture of indigenous and Spanish blood was now defending Nicaragua. Because, what would have happened in Nicaragua, imagine, if the English came and swept out the Spaniards, and finished sweeping out the Nicaraguans too?

So, the Nicaraguans are then beginning to defend their indigenous-Spanish identity, that's why Darío knows so well how to describe that mixture, and the achievements, the Human Strength of those Families, those Nicaraguans who gave Battle at San Jacinto and who were reinforced by the Indian archers, who had no Spanish blood, but 100% indigenous blood, but as Good Patriots, Brave Heroes, they gave battle there to defeat the invading Yankee. I must now conclude with these data that are incredible, incredible!

When the Yankees invaded Nicaragua in 1855, and were defeated by the Nicaraguans who would neither surrender, nor sell out, by José Dolores Estrada, Andrés Castro and the Indian archers, in that year Nicaragua had a population of 214,000 inhabitants, you see, we were a tiny people, invaded by a Country, the United States of North America, which, in that year, 1855, already had a population of 27 million inhabitants. 27 million against 200,000 Nicaraguans, and it wasn't 200,000, it was fewer those who fought, because others betrayed us.

Then in the year 1912, which is when the Yankees carried out their coup against Zelaya, Nicaragua had a population of 554,000 inhabitants, and the United States had a population of 95.3 million inhabitants. I mean, it's amazing how a country with such a population, the United States was already then a Power.

Then, in 1926, when the Yankees invaded Nicaragua, at that time Nicaragua had a population of 687,000 inhabitants, the United States then had a population of 117 million inhabitants; that is to say, we did not reach even a million people and they already had 117 million, well armed, the US was a great Power.

That's when the battle with Sandino was being fought, and they couldn't defeat Sandino, even though they brought aviation, they brought troops, they continuously brought more troops; it was 7 or 8 eight years of war, but they couldn't prevails and had to withdraw, they were defeated in Nicaragua.

Then in 1980 there was the war, after the Triumph in 1979, but the Yankees continued the war, a war that was contested, when even the President of the United States said that he too was a Contra, and he sought funds so as to give weapons, training and everything to the Counterrevolution, who were sent to train there to the United States and also to train some military bases they had installed in Honduras. He gave tremendous armament... Just imagine!

In the year 1980, the war began and lasted 10 years, many of you participated as "Cub troops”, yes, I'm sure some of you were "Cubs”, and the “Cubs" were those young men who neither sold out nor surrendered and who knew how to defend the Nation

10 years of war, and Reagan even resorted to working with drug traffickers so as to give the Contras weapons, it was a scandal back in the United States, but nothing happened as a result. But they couldn't prevail, yes, they couldn't defeat the Sandinista Army, they couldn't defeat the “cubs” and it was another defeat for Imperialism, and another victory for the Nicaraguan people.

So, beloved young men and women, our beloved Young People, we have Peace, but that does not mean that the enemy is resting, the enemy is always plotting, he is always trying to provoke bloodshed, trying to provoke pain, grief, suffering, in Nicaragua’s Families, because some people think they can overthrow the Revolution because they have the support of the Imperialists of the Earth.

That's why we have to always keep up with all the tasks that we have to fulfill, via study, preparation, work in different activities, without neglecting anything, there in the place where we are working, there in the neighborhood where we are at work, without neglecting Revolutionary Vigilance, such that there is no space left for terrorists, conspirators, sellouts, because they will know that as soon as they are discovered, they will be captured and prosecuted.

We appreciate so much the participation of the Delegations that have come from Fraternal Peoples, from Fraternal Countries, where too these Fraternal Peoples are fighting for Peace, with us here China is the greatest Power and fighting for Peace; the Russian Federation is another great Power, but it has no other option but to continue giving battle to achieve Peace, yes, but not at the cost of ceding even an inch of land to the Nazi-Fascists.

And the People's Republic of China, the Russian Federation, have been very clear, they want Peace for the World, they promote Peace for the World, they promote Solidarity for the World, they promote cooperation for the world’s Peoples; that is to say, they are noble Peoples and they are not Powerful Nations wanting to be occupying Powers seeking only how to oppress, crush and enslave.

Slavery has now been buried forever, and even with the difficult world situation, with Powers such as the People's Republic of China and the Russian Federation, who make their way in search of Peace, promoting Peace, we are sure that they are Powers whom, those who are sowing terrorism around the world, have to respect.

In the end, Peace will have to come, Peace will have to come! and all these complex, difficult, terrible situations that are being experienced, this United Nations will have to come to an end and another United Nations body will have to be created, a new United Nations, capable of being an instruments that does give security to all Nations, able to be an Instrument able to go where others are killing People, and stop those crimes.

We, of course will continue to fight here for Latin American and Caribbean Unity, we have to continue fighting for Latin American and Caribbean Unity; continue fighting to strengthen ALBA, the Bolivarian Alliance of the Peoples of Our America, and working together as close as we can, despite whatever differences we may have.

As small countries we are especially obliged to get closer, to improve the living conditions of our Peoples; otherwise, simply, if there is no rapprochement, if there is no dialogue, if there is no respect for the Agreements we reach, then simply some lose out more than others; we all lose, but some lose more than others. And we are here not for anyone to lose, but because all the Central American, Latin American and Caribbean Countries have managed to unite in the struggle for Peace, for Unity for the Well-being of our Peoples, ending Poverty.
