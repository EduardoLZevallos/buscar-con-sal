Trade Union Delegation, March 2024

Casa Ben Linder are organising a trade union delegation to Nicaragua from 2-10 March 2024. The delegation will include the following activities: -

* Meeting with unions and labour activists in all sectors from teachers to motorcycle taxis
* Tour of new hospitals & maternal wait homes with health care workers union
* Meeting with union representatives in the National Assembly
* Visit to unionised street vendors at work
* Visit to a free trade zone factory & meeting with unionised workers
* Visit to low-income housing projects
* Visit to a women’s coffee co-op
* Visit to a women’s police station
* Visit to the Masaya volcano at night and tour of bubbling lava with unionised park guides

The cost of the delegation is US $975 (ca. £750) per person for internal costs including all breakfasts and lunches, hotels, translation and entrance fees. Evening meal and airfare are separate.

If anyone from your region or branch wishes to participate in the delegation, you can do so by emailing casabenjaminlinder@gmail.com. See also https://www.casabenjaminlinder.org/tours for more information about Casa Benjamin Linder. Deadline for applications is 30 January 2024. If anyone is intending to participate, please let us know and we can help with the arrangements and answer any questions you may have.

Advances in Electricity Coverage

National electricity coverage in Nicaragua was 99.37% at the close of October. Between January 2nd and October 31st this year, 186 electrification projects were carried out, benefiting 40,782 inhabitants. Under Nicaragua’s Sandinista government, electricity coverage has expanded from 54% in 2007 to 99.37% nationwide at the end of October. This year’s goal is to close with 99.4% electricity coverage, reaching more than 1,280,000 homes, which would benefit more than 3,686,000 inhabitants. Renewable energy makes up 60.43% of the total: solar energy (0.41%); hydroelectric (7.55%); geothermal (13.40%); biomass (11.60%); wind (12.58%); thermal (39.57%), and imported energy represents 14.88%. Monroe Doctrine Weakens as Latin America Moves Forward
The Monroe Doctrine, issued by US President James Monroe on Dec. 2, 1823, stated that any interference in the Americas by a European power would be viewed as a hostile act by the United States. In 1904, President Theodore Roosevelt added the Roosevelt Corollary, which stated that the United States could become involved in a Latin American country’s internal affairs in cases of “wrongdoing” by that Latin American country. In a recent interview, Nicaragua’s Foreign Minister Denis Moncada stated that “The Monroe Doctrine is in a process of weakening, to the extent that our peoples and our governments are advancing in this struggle against imperialism, against fascism, against neocolonialism. The Monroe Doctrine, which has been the instrument of U.S. imperialism, is declining as the hegemony of the empire is declining.” To read the full interview see here

Nicaragua confirms withdrawal from Organisation of American States (OAS)

On 20 November, Nicaragua confirmed its withdrawal from the OAS. On behalf of the government, Foreign Minister Denis Moncada stated that the OAS ‘continues to be a ministry of colonies, designed and organised by the US to cover up and justify its aggressive actions against progressive nations’. He also referred to the interventionist actions of the OAS, transgressing the principles of non-intervention in internal affairs with respect to the sovereignty of States and the right of the people to choose their own destiny, actions demonstrated in Nicaragua during the failed coup attempt of 2018.
