Cuba Update - September 20th 2019

Submitted bytortilla onVie, 20/09/2019 - 10:48
 

Twitter blocks Cuban news accounts

On 11 September, Twitter blocked the accounts of leading Cuba media outlets, journalists and prominent personalities just as the Cuban president was due to appear on television to inform the public on measures adopted in the face of severe fuel shortages.

René González and Gerardo Hernández, members of the Miami Five, also announced that they had had their accounts suspended by Twitter. Cuba’s Union of Journalists released a statement condemning the action.

• Read the full statement from the Cuban Union of Journalists

 

 
 

Cuba says it is battling acute fuel shortages due to US sanctions

Cuban President Miguel Díaz-Canel announced that the country would have to implement emergency measures to stave off an acute fuel shortage in September caused by the Trump administration’s efforts to block oil shipments to the country.

Speaking during a round table discussion broadcast on national television he said the island will have to make do this month with the little fuel now available, plus the supply from two shipments arriving later in the month. All shipments for October were already guaranteed he announced.

Cuba denounces US plan to sabotage international medical volunteers

Unions for Cuba Conference

Saturday 2 November 2019 at NEU Hamilton House, London, WC1H 9BB

The Unions for Cuba conference is the highlight of Cuba60 – CSC’s year of activities to mark the 60th anniversary of the Cuban Revolution and the 80th anniversary of the CTC.

The conference comes at a critical time for the Cuban people, as the Trump administration has tightened the blockade to unprecedented levels in an attempt to strangle the Cuban economy.

Speakers include Len McCluskey (GS Unite the Union), Kevin Courtney (GS NEU), Carmen Rosa López, Deputy General Secretary, Central de Trabajadores de Cuba (CTC), Karen Lee MP, Dave Ward (GS, CWU), Steve Gillan (GS,POA), and general secretaries and trade union leaders from Cuba’s SNTS (health), SNTAP (public administration), SNTECD (education) SNTCIE (communications), SNTTP (transport and ports) and SNTEM (energy and mining) unions. Final speakers and details will be announced soon.
