Act in Commemoration of the 47th Anniversary
of the Transit to Immortality of Comandante Carlos Fonseca Amador,
Comandante in Chief of the Sandinista Popular Revolution

November 8, 2023
 

Address by Darling Ríos
National Secretary of the Sandinista Youth

Good evening also to our Nicaraguan families. Today, on the 47th Anniversary of the Transit to Immortality of the Father of the Sandinista Popular Revolution, Comandante Carlos, the July 19th Sandinista Youth, offers its commitment.

The Revolutionary Commitment of the July 19th Sandinista Youth, united for a Nicaragua, blessed and always free.

A Sandinista Youth building the Future, an heir to Peace, following in the footsteps of the Light and Truth of our Commander Carlos Fonseca Amador, namely Nation and Revolution, we firmly commit ourselves today, tomorrow and always, with the Spirit of the Revolution in our hearts, to continue defending and protecting the greatest treasure that Nicaraguan Families have, which is Peace.

We are a generation that inherits responsibly, continuing the legacy of courage and commitment of our Heroes and Martyrs, the legacy left to us by our General Sandino, our Comandante Carlos, it is the legacy transmitted to us by our Comandante Daniel, our Compañera Rosario.

Today, November 8th, the day that Comandante Carlos transcended to Immortality, the July 19th Sandinista Youth commit ourselves, through education we will continue his Great Dream: "And also, teach them to read”, but beyond teaching people to read, to show young people that the route of Education is also the route to the Future, to Peace and Prosperity.

We will continue, with great pride and dedication, to promote our Culture, our Identity, who we are, what defines us as Nicaraguans, to preserve and share for future generations our history of struggle and defense of our Sovereignty.

You taught us to love our Mother Earth, our forests, and today we are committed to protecting and preserving our natural resources from the mountains that saw you born, we will continue forward in every corner of our nation.

In the area of communication we are committed to continue promoting your message of Love, of the defense of our Sovereignty, rejecting any form of interventionism promoted by Imperialism, and ensuring that Nicaragua remains Free, carrying high the feeling of Revolutionary Unity and celebrating every day the Victories of our People.

In sport and in the practice of a healthy life, we continue to fulfill your great dream of a Nicaragua that triumphs, highlighting our athletes and accompanying them with Love and Joy for every young person, the pride of our country, in their constant search to cover our nation with glory.

Solidarity programs and Love for families, will always be and are for this Sandinista Youth, the sign of the feeling of Sister and Brotherhood among Nicaraguans, permanently accompanying the children, our elderly and our People.

Today, the Sandinista Youth, this Nicaraguan Youth, commit ourselves to continue promoting Love of our neighbor and the defense of Peace and National Sovereignty, hand in hand with Comandante Daniel and Compañera Rosario Murillo.

Comandante Carlos, here are your children, living up to your legacy of Love and Well-being for your People.

Long live Comandante Carlos!

Long live the Sandinista National Liberation Front!

Long live Comandante Daniel Ortega!

Long live Compañera Rosario

And long live the July 19th Sandinista Youth!

“He embraced me, (he was now saying goodbye to Carlos) and it was an embrace forever. And now that I reflect on my memories of that moment, so full of sadness, I find that it curious: seven Colleagues saying goodbye, at seven pm on a November 7th. In the rain that fell without mercy."

That is, they are all together, and at 7 o'clock at night Carlos already told them you go there, I am going to continue here... At 7 o'clock at night 7 Colleagues who are saying goodbye, and it's November 7th, worth noting... 3 times 7!

"Nor do I forget his demeanor when he left: The months-old underdeveloped beard, his thick glasses that were so necessary for his myopia, his olive-green uniform, his high boots, his 12-gauge automatic shotgun, his Browning 9-millimeter 14-shot pistol and a fragmentation grenade at his belt.” That's the armament Carlos had at that time.

"He went out at night, because although the problem with his eyesight made it difficult for him to walk in the dark, the first stretch would have to be done along a busy road, before entering the mountain through an opening.” Openings are the little paths up into the mountains, ways into the mountains. “The path where the enemy was precisely waiting for him”, that is, he took the path where the ambush was, already following his footsteps, they had already detected him, they were already following him... "The path where the enemy was precisely waiting for him, already behind his footsteps, sniffing in the air that Carlos Fonseca, who had been left for dead many other times, was in the mountains.” Because so many times they had left Carlos for dead.

"So it was that he fell in Boca de Piedra, Zinica Region, that same night of November 7th 1976, surprised by a patrol of Guards a few hours march from El Varillal, where we had said goodbye, without me being able to hear the shots due to the noise of the downpour of rain that continued to fall until dawn. He fought at a disadvantage. And another compañero known as 114 fell with him, and Danilo, the other peasant compañero, was able to escape, but later he was ambushed on another stretch of road, and they killed him too.”

"Between 9am and 11am the next day, as we were moving away from El Varillal, we heard gunshots coming from Boca de Piedra, accompanied by explosions. Maybe that was when they were killing the Peasant.” That is also where it is thought that Carlos was being killed. “I don't know. We never got to find out what happened to the documents I had placed under his shirt, if he managed to hide them while he was fleeing, or if the Guard found it on his body.

Carlos Fonseca went straight to his death, obsessed by the idea of an impossible meeting that occupied his mind, half blind, guided by a child in the rain in the enclosed night of the mountain and surrounded by the worst omens and murderous patrols, helicopters, dogs of prey, the regions strewn with the dead, the razed ranches and crops, every inch of road guarded, the National Guard camping in the chapels and the hermitages, when everyone denounced us out of fear and very few dared to collaborate.

And so he never came to the plains of the Iyás River, there the meeting never took place that preoccupied him so much and that cost him his life.”

That's how Comandante Francisco Rivera said goodbye to our brother Carlos, that's how he said farewell.

Look at what a dramatic description, what a harsh, terrible situation, but there was the Consciousness, the Love of the Nation, Love of Sovereignty, Love of Freedom, the Love of the People and the commitment to fight to achieve Freedom and with Freedom, Peace for this People. But Peace with Well-being, because there can be no Peace without Well-being, which is what we are engaged in now.

And we tell Carlos that there is a People here and there is a Youth that multiplies everywhere, a Youth that is carrying out Days of Service, of Care, of Cooperation with the People and especially to the humblest, the most impoverished, because it is the President Youth that serves the People too, the President Youth serving the People who have many needs, as we all know very well.

And with this Youth that is being forged, take a good look at this story that I have read to you, of the conditions in which Carlos then made his Transition to Immortality, I am sure that you carry that disposition, that courage, that bravery inside, you carry it in your soul, and that you are willing, if necessary, to give your very life for the Nation, for Freedom, for Sovereignty, for Nicaragua.

Carlos Fonseca is of the Fallen who live Forever!
