Webinar November 17th 2024 - Rural Workers' Association: Food Sovereignty, environmental protections, and labor rights

Enviado por tortilla el Vie, 15/11/2024 - 06:35
NICARAGUA WEBINAR, SUNDAY NOVEMBER 17
Noon Pacific time / 2 pm Nicaragua /
3 pm Eastern / 8 pm Greenwich UK
 
REGISTER:  bit.ly/NicaNov17
 
 
Join us to learn about the ATC, the Rural Workers Association in Nicaragua, which is achieving food sovereignty, environmental protections, and labor rights, in solidarity with and support from the Sandinista Popular Revolution.  Featured speakers this month are volunteers with the Friends of the ATC network, who will describe how the network was formed and share first-hand accounts of the profound changes that have taken place in the Nicaraguan countryside in the past 45 years.
 
Today, Nicaragua is 90% self-sufficient in food, mainly grown by small farmers who received land during the 1980s agrarian reform and who are supported today by government programs focused on the popular economy. Testimonies of visitors to Nicaragua with Friends of ATC will share how this beautiful country is on the cutting edge of a new world based upon social justice and respect for Mother Earth.
 
Speakers:
 
Michelle Munjanattu, trade unionist and internationalist living in NYC; observer of the 2021 Nicaraguan elections; organizer of comedy fundraisers to support Nicaraguan farmers.
 
(Pre-recorded video message) Ari Gutman, Buenos Aires-based master's student studying China Linkage Policies; regular visitor to Nicaragua and intern with the Friends of the ATC for four months in 2021
 
Abraham Marquez, freelance writer from Inglewood whose work covers social movements, sports, politics, and immigration; member, National Association of Hispanic Journalists (NAHJ); 2021 Fellow, USC Annenberg Center for Health Journalism
 
Alvaro Mense, educator living in St. Louis, whose political work is grounded in abolition, anti-imperialism, and decolonization; volunteer in May 2024 at the IALA institute of the ATC, while living with his maternal family in Nicaragua as a part of his language recuperation journey
 
Angel Ponce, anti-imperialist organizer based in Los Angeles whose passion for the arts serves as a dialogue between community healing and raising political consciousness
 
The webinar will be in English only, but with an option for translated captions in Zoom.  The event will be co-moderated by Paolo Cruzalegui and Barbara Larcom, and hosted by Nicaragua Solidarity Coalition, NicaSolidarity.com.
