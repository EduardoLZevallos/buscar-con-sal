Central Ceremony in Commemoration of the 41st Anniversary of Foundation of the Nicaraguan Army

September 2, 2020

Words of Rosario

Reading of Presidential Agreement 105-2020

 

 

Good afternoon. "Presidential Agreement No. 105-2020,” in which the President of the Republic of Nicaragua, Comandante Daniel Ortega Saavedra,

 

CONSIDERING :

 

1. That the process of institutional strengthening of the Nicaraguan Army makes it necessary to systematically apply the Fundamental Principles that govern the Military Career and especially to ensure the hierarchical correspondence of positions within the Superior Command and the Command Units with the effective Military Rank of the incumbents who perform within them.

 

2. That in accordance with the Norms that govern the Military Career and Ranks, Colonel Guillermo Patricio López, complies with the requirements and parameters established and has sufficient merits to be promoted to the immediate higher Military Grade.

 

3. That article 6, subsection 7, of Law N° 181 “Code of Military Organization, Jurisdiction and Social Welfare”, establishes that it is the responsibility of the President of the Republic to approve and grant the Military Degrees of General or its equivalent at the proposal of the Military Council.

 

In use of the powers conferred by the Political Constitution, the President of the Republic,
 

AGREES:

 

Article 1. Promote Colonel Guillermo Patricio López to the Military Rank of Brigadier General.

 

Article 2. This Agreement takes effect as of this date, and is published in La Gaceta, Official Gazette.

 

Given in Managua, Office of the President of the Republic of Nicaragua, on September 2, 2020.

 

Daniel Ortega Saavedra

President of the Republic"

 

 

 

Brigadier General Bayardo Pulido Ortiz

Words of Rosario

Reading of Presidential Agreement 103-2020

 

Again, good afternoon.

 

"Presidential Agreement 103-2020.” The President of the Republic, in accordance with the powers conferred by the Political Constitution of the Republic of Nicaragua,

 

CONSIDERING:

 

1. That the Nicaraguan Army, upon reaching 41 Years since its Foundation, has fulfilled with formidable Merits, its Missions of Safeguarding and Defending the Sovereign Security of Our Motherland;

 

2. That in the performance of all its Tasks and Missions it has guaranteed the Right for Sovereign Security, at all times and in all circumstances, to all the People, Families, Regions and Communities of our Nicaragua;

 

3. That their tenacious, unwavering and consistent duty, proclaimed firmness and cohesion, has been recognized by the entire People of Nicaragua, who feel their presence in all Tasks of Prevention, Attention and Response to the threats of Organized Crime, Drug Trafficking, Crime; guarding of the Air, Maritime and Land Borders; Community Action against Disasters and Calamities. Our People also recognize their active duty towards contributing to Health, Education, Electoral Processes and Security in all areas of National Production;

 

4. That the Nicaraguan Army, Worthy Successor of the Army for the Defense of National Sovereignty, of our General Augusto Sandino, and historical continuation of the Sandinista Popular Army, celebrates 41 Years of commendable work at the service of all Nicaraguan Families, without any distinction;

 

THEREFORE,

 

The President of the Republic,

 

Resolves:

 

1. Deliver to the Nicaraguan Army, its Commander in Chief, General Command, Military Council, Officers and Soldiers, on this 41st Anniversary, the Augusto Sandino Order in its Highest Degree, Battle of San Jacinto, in the person of the Army General, Julio César Avilés Castillo, Commander in Chief of the Nicaraguan Army;

 

2. Support and fully recognize all the Missions that are developed and will continue to be developed with the Patriotism and Courage of the Best Children of our Motherland, in defense of National Sovereignty, Nature, and the Dignified Life of the entire Nicaraguan People,

 

3. Render Honor and Glory to the thousands of Nicaraguan Sisters and Brothers who gave their lives in defense of National Sovereignty, Justice and Peace. Likewise, express all our Solidarity and Recognition to the Retired Sisters and Brothers of the Army, and those who resulted with different disabilities, victims of the terrorist interventions and practices of the aggressor Powers;

 

4. Ratify our Respect, Love and Solidarity to the Families of these Sisters and Brothers who have been victims of State Terrorism from the same aggressor Powers.

 

Given in the City of Managua on the 2nd day of the Month of September of the Year 2020.

 

Daniel Ortega Saavedra

President of the Republic of Nicaragua"

Nicaraguans; Brothers in Arms;

Sisters and Brothers:

 

Today, September 2, we are commemorating the 41st Anniversary of the Constitution of the Nicaraguan Army. We do it always giving the maximum of our efforts to serve our People in the best possible way, without distinctions of any nature.

 

As we commemorate our Anniversary, we are honoring September 2, 1927, the date of the Foundation of the Army for the Defense of National Sovereignty, commanded by General Sandino. The Army for the Defense of National Sovereignty of which we are Worthy Heirs of its motto of Motherland and Freedom; Worthy Heirs of his Fight for Independence, Sovereignty and National Self-determination.

 

The Constitution of the Republic mandates that the Nicaraguan Army is the Institution of the Nation to defend Sovereignty, Independence and Territorial Integrity. A Sacred Mission that we fulfill with a high Sense of Patriotism, Firmness and Cohesion, from the actions carried out by our Members in the Land, Air and Naval Components. All of them, with great discipline and courage, guaranteeing the protection of our Land and Maritime Borders and the control of our Airspace, enforcing the Nicaraguan Law.

 

We continue making significant contributions to the Safety of our People. We continue to be the Safest Country in the Region as we do not have any Gangs or Cartels. We have no clandestine landing strips and we are not a country used for the storage of drug trafficking. We do not allow illegal migration, and we maintain strict controls and cooperation to prevent the transit of individuals linked to Terrorist Organizations.

 

We have excellent Security Levels in our Motherland, based on the successful National Retaining Wall Strategy, a Strategy that ensures the containment, divertion and capture of drugs, criminals, resources and means immediately as they access the National Territory.

 

We also firmly contribute, in coordination with different Institutions, to maintain our Ports and Airports with high levels of Security, this is certified by International Organizations for the import and export of merchandise and the transit of people, which benefits the economical activities in the Country. And we continue to contribute to Security in the Rural Ares, so that our Producers, Farmers and Ranchers can develop their productive activities with Tranquility and Peace. To them, our permanent commitment to continue supporting them from our Territorial Units.

 

Nicaraguans:

 

In this 2020 the World has been affected by a terrible pandemic, COVID-19. According to figures from International Organizations, more than 25 million people have been affected, and the figures for deaths exceed 800,000. The WHO, on January 30, released the World Health Alert and on March 11 declared the situation of COVID-19 as a Pandemic.

 

Faced with this situation, within the Nicaraguan Army we proceeded to suspend a large number of activities planned to be carried out this year, focusing our main efforts on the Missions related to the protection of the Sovereignty and Security of the Country, and redirecting all efforts towards the compliance of tasks for prevention, and Actions against COVID-19.

 

In this regard, since January 31, our Military Medical Corps Staff have been part of the Monitoring and Follow-up Team on the situation caused by COVID-19.

 

In the Nicaraguan Army we do not improvise in these situations. We do not improvise because we have a Disaster Plan approved by the President of the Republic. And as part of SINAPRED, we are prepared for different risk situations for our population, having updated Response Plans for different situations, such as earthquakes, tsunamis, eruptions, hurricanes, large fires, among other situations, and of course, Anti-Epidemic or Pandemics Plans.

 

This allows us to have clearly established Courses of Action to be followed by the General Staff and the different Components of the Nicaraguan Army.

 

What did we do at first? We reoriented all our Planning to the current situation; to clarify from the Military Medical Corps the risk levels for the Country and we designed the Plans for assesing and preparing the Medical Healthcare Personnel, and from these, the preparation of all our Military Units. 2,220 Members of the Military Medical Corps were appointed to the Military Hospital and the 76 Medical and Healthcare Posts, to contribute to the prevention and care of the population.

 

From the beginning, we contributed to providing awareness about the risks, and Measures to adopt in the face of the Pandemic, following the Recommendations of the National Authorities and the WHO, preparing multiple videos and documents to broadcast inside and outside the Military Units.

 

We started the preparation of our Military Personnel for the creation of trained Contingents to carry out actions for the management and elimination of bio-infectious waste, and the disinfection of sites and public areas.

 

We reoriented the Production Plans of the Military Industry, prioritizing the manufacture of garments, such as masks, protective shields, protective clothing and garments for hospitals, as well as special suits for the Disinfection Days. To date, more than 65,000 resources have been produced.

 

Within the Engineering Corps, with our Chemical Specialists, in coordination with the Military Medical Corps, a Work Team was organized for the production of substances for disinfection, producing to date 8,000 gallons of Chlorine for hands and surfaces; 1,000 gallons of liquid soap and alcohol gel, aswell as 200,000 gallons of hydrogen peroxide.

 

The Military Medical Corps, in compliance with the Special Pandemic Plan for the Prevention, Control and Containment, implemented Protocols for the Care of Users with high levels of security in the Military Hospital. We re-elaborated the Missions to be fulfilled by the 6 Campaign Hospitals, to be used in cases of need, deploying one of them and designating the Facilities of a Military Unit as a reserve for medical care.

 

As a measure against the pandemic, we reinforced the Borders with 1,000 Military Personnel, with the aim of preventing the movement of people through non-authorized entry points, and for this, we established coordinations with neighboring Armed Forces.

 

We also contribute to the implementation of Prevention and Security Measures in our Ports and Airports together with Health Authorities.

 

In a second stage, from the month of May, authorized by the President of the Republic, and in coordination with National, Departmental, Regional and Municipal Authorities, we iniciated the Disinfection Days with Contingents of Forces integrated by the different Military Units.

 

To date, we have carried out intense Disinfection Days covering the most populated areas of our Country, which we have carried out in all the Districts of Managua and the most populated Municipalities, Ciudad Sandino and Tipitapa; Chinandega, León, Masaya, Granada, Nandaime, Jinotepe, Diriamba, San Marcos, Rivas and San Juan del Sur, Matagalpa and Sébaco, Estelí, Somoto, Ocotal, Jinotega, Boaco, Juigalpa, Bluefields, Bilwi, and we concluded this First Stage on August 28, in San Carlos, Río San Juan.

 

We emphasize that within these intense days of Disinfection we have covered 91 Medical Assistance Centers, 73 Educational and Cultural Centers, 43 Markets and Commercial Facilities, 283 places with high circulation of people, 145 Public Buildings with large influx of persons, as well as Transportation Terminals, roads and Bus Stops with high pedestrian traffic, among other areas.

 

The Disinfection Days to date have reached 6 million 300,000 square meters, benefiting more than 2 million people.

 

In this Solidarity, Disciplined and Patriotic Work we have involved, on a rotating basis, up to 6,000 Military Personnel, 700 vehicles including special, cargo, light, naval and aerial means. A total of 1,500 individual pumps and 150,000 gallons of hydrogen peroxide have been used.

 

All the efforts that we have made and those that we will continue to do to face the Pandemic are dedicated to the Memory of the Nicaraguans affected by COVID, and to the Heroic Doctors and Health Personnel in general, who from the first line of fighting against the Pandemics have saved thousands of Nicaraguan Lives.

 

Today in this Ceremony, we make a deserved recognition to all the Sisters and Brothers in Arms and Units in general, who with dedication are contributing in this fight against the Pandemic.

 

Our Special Recognition to the General Staff, the Military Medical Corps, the Infantry Brigade, the Civil Defense General Staff, the Humanitarian and Rescue Unit, the Military Industry, the Engineering Corps, the National School of Sergeants, the Command Regiment and the Military Police, and Units that have participated in all the Disinfection Days.

 

President, we are ready to continue the Second Stage of Disinfection as of the second week of September, awaiting your command to restart here in the City of Managua.

 

Along with all these efforts, we have carried out 103 Search and Rescue Missions, managing to rescue 165 Nicaraguan Sisters ans Brothers alive; Rescue Missions where our Staff risks their Lives to save others, such as the rescue of 10 people carried out by our Heroic Sailors, on August 9 in our Caribbean Sea, where one of our boats with 5 crew members who participated in the Mission is still missing, and since that date we have not stopped the search operations.

 

These informations reported to the Nation are part of the foundation of saying that we are the People itself in Uniform, working for its own benefit, and in the Nicaraguan Army, we say what we do and we do what we say.

 

We continue to advance in our Modernization and Development, strengthening our capabilities to be in better conditions to serve our People.

 

International Cooperation is relevant for security purposes and the possibility of acquiring Means that the Nation is not yet able to supply. In this regard, we inform that with the support of the Russian Federation and the International Civil Defense Organization, we continue to strengthen the country's response capabilities to face disaster situations, with the modernization of the Disaster Operations Center of our Civil Defense.

 

A helicopter equipped to extinguish forest fires is already in our country, and our Pilots will be receiving the corresponding training in the upcoming months. Also, we are already in the process of receiving the necessary means for the installation of the second Early Warning System for Earthquakes and Tsunami, which will be destined for the Caribbean of Nicaragua.

 

Our gratitude to the Russian Federation and the International Civil Defense Organization for their significant support, without conditions of any kind.

 

Equal thanks to Taiwan for supporting the Military Industry Production Plans. To Mexico for supporting us in the preparation of Military Medical Specialists.

 

We also thank the Friendly Armed Forces for the support provided, and the Members of the Central American Armed Forces Conference for their excellent Friendship and Cooperation Relationships. At CFAC we are and we will continue to highly cooperate on different issues for the Well-being of our Nations.

 

Today, 313 Officers have been promoted for complying with the parameters established in the Military Career. These Degrees are an expression of Discipline, Dedication and Loyalty to the Motherland. Our congratulations to Brigadier General Guillermo Patricio López and Colonels Vanegas, Domínguez, Rugama, Castro, López, García, Urbina, Soza, Rueda, and Captain Berríos.

 

We also congratulate those promoted in the Superior and Subaltern Official Degrees, and especially the 24 Sisters in Arms who were promoted today. Congratulations to you all.

 

Sisters and Brothers in Arms, in this Ceremony for the 41st Anniversary of Foundation of the Nicaraguan Army, we wish to highlight that in fulfillment of our Missions and Tasks during the reporting period, our Land Forces performed more than 230,000 Operational Services; our Naval Force sailed over 1,130,000 miles, and our Air Force flew over 900 hours.

 

These figures, together with other reported data, are a clear reflection of the unvaring commitment of those of us who integrate this Institution at the service of all Nicaraguans. For this reason, we meritoriously acknowledge the work carried out by the General Corps, the Senior and Junior Officers, as well as the NCOs, Officials, Sergeants, Soldiers, Pilots, Sailors and Auxiliary Personnel.

 

Everyone, from the General Staff, types of Forces, Central Subordination Units, Common and Supportive Bodies, and Territorial Units, demonstrate, with concrete facts, that we are firm and highly cohesive in the defense of our Motherland and Institution.

 

Also, on this 41st Anniversary it is fair to recognize the contributions of the Sisters and Brothers in Retirement status and reaffirm our Commitment and Honor to the Fallen, to always be loyal to our Beloved Motherland, strictly complying with what the Constitution and Laws mandate.

 

In this Solemn Ceremony we wisth to thank our People for the trust they have put in this Institution that belongs to them and that they nurture with their children to serve them.

 

President, on behalf of all the Members of the Nicaraguan Army, we want to thank you for your support to be in a better position to serve all Nicaraguans, without distinction of any kind. Thank you, President, for your strong support.

In a special way, the gratitude to our Relatives, their support strengthens us to fulfill our Sacred Missions.

 

Brothers in Arms, 41 Years at the service of our People, 41 Years fulfilling our Missions with Patriotism, Dignity and Courage, 41 Years giving everything for the Motherland, where thousands of Brothers in Arms offered their lives thinking of the Free, Dignified, Just and Prosperous Nicaragua that we all deserve. In their honor, we ask for a minute of silence.

 

All Nicaraguans, the Members of the Nicaraguan Army are firmly committed to contributing so that together we will always have our Motherland with Security, Stability, Tranquility, and the greatest desire of all, Peace.

 

Security and Peace to work and produce. Security and Peace to produce and progress. Security, Stability, Tranquility and Peace, Everyone's Guarantee to live in Harmony, with National Development and Well-being.

 

Brothers in arms, let us always remain firm and cohesive, defending our Sovereignty, Independence and National Self-determination, always firm and cohesive, guaranteeing our Territorial Integrity, always firm and cohesive to guarantee Security, Stability, Tranquility and Peace. Always firm and highly cohesive in defense of the Motherland and the Institution.

Words of Daniel

 

Nicaraguan brothers; Families ... All Nicaraguan Families are part of a Great Family, which is Nicaragua, and Nicaragua at the same time is part of another Great Family, which is the Central American, Mesoamerican, Latin American and Caribbean Family. And precisely today, September 2, beginning of the Month of the Motherland, beginning the Month in which Central America will be reaching, this next September 15, 199 Years of its Independence, and we will be, therefore, on the eve of the Bicentennial. In 2021 it will be 200 Years of the Independence of Central America.

 

Our Affection, our Solidarity, our Love, for the Families and Brothers, Friggite Luitenant Freddy Gómez Álvarez, originally from San Miguelito, Río San Juan; Sailor Carlos Daniel Calero, originally from Bluefields; Sailor Alex Antonio Álvarez Mendoza, originally from Managua, Jorge Casaya neighborhood; Sailor Jaime Jaimard Taylor, originally from Bluefields; Sailor Rolando Tangeris Salinas, originally from Bilwi or Puerto Cabezas.

 

Since August 9, these Brothers are missing having disappeared when they extended their helping hands to other Nicaraguan Brothers who were already shipwrecked and who were all going to perish. There were 12 Nicaraguan Brothers who were at that time in the Pearl Lagoon Bar, in that area, there are rough waters, and 12 Brothers about to lose their lives, when the Sailor Brothers of the Naval Force arrived to rescue them.

 

They did not leave the site, even with the risks involved in staying in that place, advancing, to rescue these 12 Brothers. And they managed to rescue 10, they saved the Life of 10 Nicaraguan Brothers; 2 died, they could not be rescued. Of the 12, 2. And they rescued 10.

 

But later, when they were searched for in the midst of the turbulence of the waters, the 5 Brothers that I have mentioned were not found, and who fulfilled their Duty as Good Soldiers, as Good Patriots, as Good Christians. And searches have been carried out by Sea, with boats of all kinds, where Armed Forces from Brotherly Countries have cooperated, which have also incorporated airships to explore from the air to see if they can find traces of our Comrades.

 

The Territory has also been traced, General Avilés told me, tracked by long walks, tracing the beach, the forest bordering the beach, looking for signs of Life of the Comrades.

 

We know that here, and in the world, accidents of this type have occurred, where even a crew has disappeared for a month or more, and then they appear far from the place where they first were.

 

We pray to God to keep them alive. And we accompany their Families, and may the Families of these Brothers, mothers, sisters, sons, daughters, who will be suffering, rest assured, that we accompany them, not only with words, but that we accompany them with all the efforts that have been made, and will continue to be made. That is, the search has not been suspended, the search is maintained, the search is extended to other points, to other sites. And we pray to God that He keeps them alive, and that they can return to their Families.

As we said, we thank God for giving us the Blessing of being here commemorating this day, in moments that are difficult, tragic, for all of Humanity. The tragedy is not only the tragedy that the Peoples of the Developing Countries have suffered and suffer historically ... How many tragedies? How many pains? How Many Epidemics? How many natural disasters? How many immigrants drowned looking for Life? We are talking about millions of Human Beings.

 

How many Children and how many Adults dying of hunger, yes, in the 21st century? Millions of Human Beings perishing from hunger. How many Children and how many Adults perishing from diseases that can be easily controlled in these Times? But since they do not exist in their countries, these Medical Services have not been developed, the Policy of selfishness has been imposed, the Policy of Wild Capitalism, where there is no Heart. What is there as services, sale of services, and if you want a Consultation, pay, and if you don't have, then leave! And you can arrive with a dying patient at the doors of a Hospital, even in Developed Countries, even in the United States of America, and if you do not have how to pay or you do not have Private Insurance, simply do not go in. This is how millions of Human Beings die in Developing Countries, and they also die in Developed Countries.

 

In the United States, as incredible as it may seem, there are millions of unemployed before the Pandemic, who go up and down there, wandering in the big cities, from barrel to barrel in New York, and looking there in the barrels to see what They find as food, or of use, to cover themselves, to do with what they can find there, pieces of cardboard or whatever, the bed where they go to sleep on the street, sleeping under bridges.
